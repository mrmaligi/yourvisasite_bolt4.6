# Subclass 173 - Contributory Parent (Temporary) Visa

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Contributory Parent (Temporary) Visa (Subclass 173)** allows parents of a settled Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen to live in Australia temporarily for up to 2 years. This visa is part of the "contributory" parent pathway where higher fees result in faster processing compared to non-contributory parent visas.

**Key Features:**
- Duration: Up to 2 years
- Work Rights: Full work rights
- Study Rights: Permitted
- Medicare: May be eligible (depending on circumstances)
- Cost: High (two-stage payment structure)
- Pathway: Can apply for Subclass 143 (permanent) to stay longer

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### Primary Requirements:
- **Parent Relationship:** Must be parent of Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- **Child Status:** Child must be "settled" in Australia (generally 2+ years residence)
- **Balance of Family Test:** At least half of children must live in Australia, or more children in Australia than any other country
- **Health & Character:** Must meet requirements
- **Assurance of Support:** Required (financial bond)
- **No Outstanding Debts:** No debts to Australian Government

### Balance of Family Test:
- At least half of children must be eligible children (living in Australia as citizens/PRs), OR
- More eligible children living in Australia than in any other single country
- Includes step-children
- Children who have passed away may be excluded from calculation

### Sponsor Requirements:
- Must be child (or spouse/de facto partner of child)
- Must be Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- Must be settled in Australia (usually 2+ years)
- Must be 18+ years
- Can be joint sponsor with another eligible child

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Confirm Eligibility
- Verify parent-child relationship
- Check child's settled status
- Confirm balance of family test met
- Review health and character requirements

### Step 2: Check Queue Position
- Parent visas subject to capping and queueing
- Applications lodged before 1 June 2018 assigned queue date
- New applications join the queue
- Check current queue release dates

### Step 3: Prepare Documents
- Identity documents
- Relationship evidence
- Sponsor documents
- Balance of family test evidence
- Health and character documents

### Step 4: Submit Application
- Complete Form 47PA
- Lodge application online or by post
- Pay first installment of visa application charge

### Step 5: Wait in Queue
- Application placed in queue
- Wait for queue date to be reached
- Respond to any requests

### Step 6: Second Installment Request
- When application reaches front of queue
- Pay second installment (large portion of total cost)
- Provide assurance of support

### Step 7: Final Processing
- Health and character checks
- Final documentation
- Visa grant

### Step 8: After Grant
- Enter Australia (if offshore)
- Can work and study
- Eligible for Medicare (conditions apply)
- Can apply for Subclass 143 for permanent stay

## 6. Processing Times (Official)

### Queue System:
Parent visas operate on a capping and queueing system:
- **Capping:** Maximum number granted each program year
- **Queueing:** Applications processed in order of queue date

### Current Queue Status (2025):
- Applications from 2017-2018 currently being processed
- New applications joining the queue
- Processing time varies based on queue position

### Estimated Timeline:
| Stage | Estimated Time |
|-------|---------------|
| **Queue Assignment** | Immediate upon lodgement |
| **Queue Waiting** | 5-7 years |
| **Final Processing** | 6-12 months |
| **Total** | 5-8 years |

### Processing Priorities:
- Contributory visas processed faster than non-contributory
- Aged parent visas may have priority
- Queue release dates published quarterly

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes:
1. **Balance of Family Test Failure** - Must meet strict requirements
2. **Insufficient Relationship Evidence** - Clear proof of parent-child relationship needed
3. **Sponsor Not Settled** - Sponsor must meet 2-year residence requirement
4. **Incorrect Fee Payment** - Must pay both installments
5. **Missing Queue Documentation** - Must respond to all requests
6. **Assurance of Support Issues** - Bond must be provided correctly

### Pro Tips:
1. **Consider 143 Direct** - May be better to apply for permanent visa directly
2. **Balance of Family Check** - Verify test met before applying
3. **Sponsor Settlement** - Ensure sponsor meets 2-year requirement
4. **Financial Preparation** - Save for high fees
5. **Assurance of Support** - Arrange bond early
6. **Queue Monitoring** - Check queue release dates regularly
7. **173 to 143 Pathway** - Apply for 143 before 173 expires to spread costs
