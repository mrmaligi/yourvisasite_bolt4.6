# Subclass 103 - Parent Visa

## Section 1: Executive Overview

The Parent visa (subclass 103) is a permanent visa that allows parents of Australian citizens, Australian permanent residents, or eligible New Zealand citizens to live in Australia permanently. This is a "non-contributory" parent visa, meaning it has a lower cost but significantly longer processing times compared to the contributory parent visas.

**Key Features:**
- **Visa Type:** Permanent Resident
- **Cost:** Lower than contributory parent visas (~AUD $7,000 total)
- **Processing Time:** 13-30+ years (currently queued at 2011)
- **Queue System:** Applications placed in queue after meeting initial criteria
- **Location:** Must apply outside Australia (offshore)

**Important Consideration:**
The long processing queue means most applicants wait 15-30 years. Consider Subclass 143 (Contributory Parent) for faster processing (5-7 years) or Subclass 870 (Temporary Parent) for temporary stays.

---

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements

### Balance of Family Test

The parent must meet the "balance of family test" - meaning:
- At least half of the parent's children live permanently in Australia, OR
- More children live permanently in Australia than in any other single country

**Example qualifying scenarios:**
- 1 child in Australia, 0 elsewhere = PASS
- 2 children in Australia, 1 in USA = PASS
- 1 child in Australia, 1 in UK = FAIL

### Sponsor Requirements

The sponsor must be:
- Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- Settled in Australia (usually 2+ years residence)
- At least 18 years old
- Child of the applicant (or child's spouse/partner)

### Health and Character

- Meet health requirements
- Meet character requirements
- Assurance of Support may be required (financial bond)

### Other Requirements

- Must apply outside Australia
- Must be outside Australia when visa is decided
- Cannot hold a Sponsored Parent (Temporary) visa (870) when applying

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Check Balance of Family Test
- Document all children's locations
- Verify at least half are in Australia

### Step 2: Verify Sponsor Eligibility
- Confirm sponsor is settled in Australia
- Gather sponsor's evidence (citizenship/residency)

### Step 3: Gather Documents
- Identity documents
- Family relationship evidence
- Children's status evidence
- Health and character documents

### Step 4: Create ImmiAccount
- Register at https://online.immi.gov.au

### Step 5: Complete Application
- Select "Parent" → "Parent (103)"
- Answer all questions
- Upload documents

### Step 6: Pay First Instalment
- Base application charge
- Additional applicant charges

### Step 7: Submit Application
- Application placed in queue
- Receive queue date

### Step 8: Wait in Queue
- Current queue: Applications from 2011 being processed
- Estimated wait: 13-30+ years

### Step 9: Request for Final Processing
- When queue date reached, DHA requests:
  - Second visa application charge
  - Health examinations
  - Police certificates
  - Assurance of Support

### Step 10: Visa Grant
- After final processing completed

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes

1. **Balance of Family Miscalculation**
   - Mistake: Not meeting the 50% requirement
   - Solution: Calculate carefully before applying

2. **Not Understanding Wait Time**
   - Mistake: Applying without realizing 15+ year wait
   - Solution: Consider Contributory Parent visa (143) for faster processing

3. **Incomplete Documents**
   - Mistake: Missing children's evidence
   - Solution: Gather all children's status documents

4. **Sponsor Not Settled**
   - Mistake: Sponsor hasn't lived in Australia long enough
   - Solution: Wait until 2 years residence

### Expert Tips

**Before Applying:**
- Seriously consider Subclass 143 (Contributory) for faster processing
- Calculate true cost difference (time value of money)
- Consider temporary parent visa (870) for immediate visits

**During Wait:**
- Keep documents updated
- Notify DHA of changes (address, new grandchildren)
- Maintain health insurance
- Consider visitor visas for temporary visits

**When Queue Date Reached:**
- Respond promptly to requests
- Prepare Assurance of Support early
- Complete health examinations quickly

### 10G. Speed-Up Options

**There is NO way to speed up the queue.**

**Alternatives to Consider:**
1. **Subclass 143 (Contributory Parent):**
   - Higher cost (~$50,000)
   - Processing: 5-7 years

2. **Subclass 870 (Temporary Parent):**
   - 3 or 5 year temporary stay
   - Processing: 3-6 months
   - No pathway to permanent

3. **Visitor Visas:**
   - Multiple entry 12-month visas
   - Processing: 1-4 weeks
   - Cannot stay permanently
