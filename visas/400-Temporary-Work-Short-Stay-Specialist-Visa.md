# Subclass 400 - Temporary Work (Short Stay Specialist) Visa

## Section 1: Executive Overview

**Visa Type:** Temporary Work Visa  
**Purpose:** Allows highly skilled workers to do short-term specialized work in Australia  
**Duration:** Up to 6 months (typically 3 months)  
**Cost:** AUD $310 (base application charge)  
**Processing Time:** 14-28 days (fast processing)

### What is the Subclass 400 Visa?

The Subclass 400 Temporary Work (Short Stay Specialist) Visa is designed for **highly skilled professionals** who need to travel to Australia for **short-term, specialized work** that cannot be done by an Australian worker. This visa is ideal for consultants, specialists, and experts brought in for specific projects or assignments.

### Key Benefits

- **Fast processing** - Typically 2-4 weeks
- **Short-term solution** - Up to 6 months (usually 3)
- **Work rights** - Can perform specialized work
- **Multiple entries** - Travel to and from Australia
- **Study permitted** - Can study for up to 3 months
- **No sponsorship required** - Different from 482 visa
- **Lower cost** - Less expensive than long-term work visas

### Important Limitations

- **Short duration only** - Maximum 6 months, usually 3
- **Specialized work only** - Cannot be for general employment
- **No pathway to PR** - Temporary only
- **Cannot extend** - Must leave when visa expires
- **No family work rights** - Family members cannot work

### Who Is This Visa For?

- Highly skilled specialists for short-term projects
- Technical experts installing specialized equipment
- Consultants providing expert advice
- Trainers conducting specialized training programs
- Professionals attending business negotiations or conferences with work component
- Emergency specialists for critical situations

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements

### Core Eligibility Criteria

#### 1. Specialized Skills
Must have **specialized skills, knowledge, or experience** that:
- Are not generally available in Australia
- Are critical to the Australian business/project
- Cannot be filled by an Australian worker

#### 2. Short-Term Need
Work must be:
- **Genuinely short-term** (typically 3 months or less)
- **Non-ongoing** position
- Specific project or assignment based
- Maximum 6 months in exceptional circumstances

#### 3. Genuine Temporary Entrant
Must demonstrate:
- Intention to stay in Australia temporarily
- Strong ties to home country
- Willingness to comply with visa conditions
- No intention to remain permanently

#### 4. Financial Capacity
Must demonstrate **ability to support yourself**:
- Sufficient funds for stay
- Or evidence of employer support
- Health insurance arrangements
- Accommodation arrangements

#### 5. Health and Character
- Must meet Australia's health standards
- Must meet character requirements
- Provide police clearances if requested

### Work Categories

#### Category 1: Specialized Work
- Highly specialized skills required
- Specific technical knowledge
- Equipment installation/maintenance
- Expert consulting services

#### Category 2: Business Activities
- Business negotiations
- Contract discussions
- Market research
- Conference participation with work component

#### Category 3: Emergency/Privileged Entry
- Emergency situations
- Government agreements
- International obligations

### What Work is NOT Allowed

- General employment
- Ongoing positions
- Work that could be done by Australian worker
- Low-skilled positions
- Construction work (generally)

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Confirm Eligibility (Week 1)
- Verify work is genuinely short-term and specialized
- Confirm skills are not available in Australia
- Check maximum stay requirement (usually 3 months)
- Gather evidence of specialized skills

### Step 2: Gather Documents (Weeks 1-2)
- Employment contract or letter from Australian company
- Evidence of specialized skills and qualifications
- Financial evidence
- Identity documents

### Step 3: Create ImmiAccount (Week 2)
- Create or access ImmiAccount
- Prepare for online application

### Step 4: Lodge Application (Week 2)
- Complete online application form
- Pay visa application charge (AUD $310)
- Upload all supporting documents
- Submit application

### Step 5: Health Examinations (Weeks 2-4)
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Book through Bupa Medical Visa Services

### Step 6: Wait for Processing (Weeks 2-4)
- Processing typically 14-28 days
- Monitor ImmiAccount for updates
- Respond promptly to any requests

### Step 7: Visa Grant
- Receive grant notification
- Check visa conditions
- Make travel arrangements
- Begin work

## Section 7: Key Considerations

### Visa Conditions

The Subclass 400 visa typically includes:
- **Condition 8101:** Work only in specified occupation/activity
- **Condition 8201:** Maximum 3 months study
- **Condition 8501:** Maintain adequate health insurance
- **Condition 8503:** No further stay (cannot extend)
- **Condition 8527:** Must maintain adequate arrangements for support
- **Condition 8531:** Must leave Australia before visa expires

### No Further Stay Condition (8503)

**Important:** Most 400 visas include a "No Further Stay" condition:
- Cannot apply for most other visas while in Australia
- Must leave Australia when visa expires
- Cannot extend stay onshore
- Limited exceptions for compelling circumstances

### Work Limitations

You must:
- Only work in the specialized occupation specified
- Only perform the activities stated in application
- Not engage in general employment
- Cease work when visa expires

### Duration Limitations

- Standard grant: Up to 3 months
- Exceptional circumstances: Up to 6 months
- Cannot extend beyond maximum
- Must depart when work complete or visa expires

### Family Members

Family members can be included but:
- Cannot work in Australia
- Can study for up to 3 months
- Must maintain health insurance

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Applying for Ongoing Position
**Problem:** Work is actually ongoing employment, not short-term specialized work.
**Solution:**
- Ensure work is genuinely temporary
- Have clear end date
- Demonstrate why it's non-ongoing
- Consider Subclass 482 if ongoing work

### Mistake 2: Insufficient Specialized Evidence
**Problem:** Not proving skills are specialized or unavailable in Australia.
**Solution:**
- Provide detailed skills evidence
- Explain why Australian worker cannot do it
- Include client/employer letter explaining need
- Show unique qualifications or experience

### Mistake 3: Wrong Visa Category
**Problem:** Applying for 400 when 482 or other visa more appropriate.
**Solution:**
- Review all work visa options
- 482 for sponsored ongoing work
- 408 for specific activities
- 400 only for short-term specialized work

### Mistake 4: Exceeding Duration
**Problem:** Work plan exceeds 3-6 month limit.
**Solution:**
- Plan work within visa duration
- Consider 482 for longer assignments
- Apply for duration needed, not maximum
- Have clear project end date

### Mistake 5: Weak GTE Evidence
**Problem:** Not demonstrating intention to leave Australia.
**Solution:**
- Show strong ties to home country
- Provide return flight booking
- Explain post-work plans
- Include employment letter for return
