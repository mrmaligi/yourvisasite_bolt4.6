# Subclass 191 - Permanent Residence (Skilled Regional) Visa

## Section 1: Executive Overview

**Visa Type:** Permanent Resident Visa  
**Purpose:** Permanent residency pathway for skilled workers who have lived and worked in regional Australia on a provisional visa  
**Duration:** Permanent - indefinite stay  
**Cost:** AUD $465 (base application charge)  
**Processing Time:** 6-12 months (90% of applications)

### What is the Subclass 191 Visa?

The Subclass 191 visa is a **permanent residency visa** designed for skilled workers who have already lived and worked in designated regional areas of Australia on an eligible provisional visa (Subclass 491 or 494). This visa represents the **final step** in the regional skilled migration pathway, granting permanent residence after meeting the 3-year regional residence and income requirements.

### Key Benefits

- **Permanent residency** - Live in Australia indefinitely
- **Work rights** - Work for any employer in any occupation
- **Medicare access** - Enroll in Australia's public healthcare system
- **Study rights** - Access domestic student rates
- **Citizenship pathway** - Eligible to apply for Australian citizenship after meeting residency requirements
- **Family sponsorship** - Sponsor eligible relatives for permanent residence
- **Travel flexibility** - Travel to and from Australia for 5 years from grant date
- **No regional restrictions** - Can live anywhere in Australia after grant

### Who Is This Visa For?

- Current Subclass 491 (Skilled Work Regional) visa holders
- Current Subclass 494 (Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional) visa holders
- Applicants who have lived in regional Australia for at least 3 years
- Applicants who can demonstrate qualifying taxable income for 3 years

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements

### Core Eligibility Criteria

#### 1. Hold an Eligible Visa
You must have held **one** of the following visas for at least 3 years:
- Subclass 491 (Skilled Work Regional Provisional)
- Subclass 494 (Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional Provisional)

#### 2. Regional Residence Requirement
- Must have **lived, worked, and studied** in a designated regional area of Australia
- Minimum **3 years** of regional residence required
- Must have complied with all visa conditions of your provisional visa

#### 3. Income Requirement
- Must provide **notices of assessment** from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
- Must demonstrate **3 years of qualifying taxable income** at or above the minimum threshold
- The minimum income threshold is updated annually and was approximately **$53,900** (check current threshold)

#### 4. Compliance with Visa Conditions
- Must have complied with all conditions of your provisional visa
- No breaches of visa conditions during the 3-year period

### Additional Requirements

#### Health Requirements
- Meet Australia's health standards
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Family members must also meet health requirements

#### Character Requirements
- Meet Australia's character requirements
- Provide police clearances if requested
- Declare any criminal history

#### Debt to Australian Government
- Must have no outstanding debts to the Australian government
- Must have arranged repayment of any outstanding debts

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Gather Evidence (Weeks 1-2)
- Collect 3 years of tax returns and notices of assessment from the ATO
- Gather evidence of regional residence (lease agreements, utility bills, employment records)
- Prepare identity documents
- Obtain health insurance records if applicable

### Step 2: Check Income Thresholds (Week 2)
- Verify your taxable income meets the minimum threshold for all 3 years
- The threshold is indexed annually - check current requirements
- If you didn't meet the threshold one year, you may need to extend your provisional visa

### Step 3: Create ImmiAccount (Week 2)
- Create or log in to your ImmiAccount
- This is where you'll lodge your application and track progress

### Step 4: Lodge Application (Week 3)
- Complete the online application form
- Pay the visa application charge (AUD $465)
- Upload all supporting documents
- Submit your application

### Step 5: Health Examinations (Weeks 3-6)
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Book through Bupa Medical Visa Services
- Results are valid for 12 months

### Step 6: Wait for Processing (Months 3-12)
- Monitor your ImmiAccount for updates
- Respond promptly to any requests for additional information
- Processing typically takes 6-12 months

### Step 7: Visa Grant
- Receive visa grant notification via email
- Check visa conditions and grant details in ImmiAccount
- Celebrate your permanent residency!

## Section 7: Key Considerations

### Understanding the Income Threshold

The income requirement is the **most critical** aspect of the 191 visa. Key points:

- **Taxable income** is what matters (not gross income)
- The threshold is indexed annually (approximately $53,900+)
- You need to meet the threshold for **3 full financial years**
- If you don't meet the threshold one year, you may need to extend your provisional visa

### Regional Areas Definition

You must have lived in a **designated regional area** which includes:
- All of Australia **except** Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane
- Perth is now considered regional (from 2022)
- Specific postcodes apply - verify your location

### Common Compliance Issues

- Working outside regional area
- Not meeting income threshold
- Breaching visa conditions
- Extended periods outside Australia

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Not Meeting Income Threshold
**Problem:** Applicants assume they qualify without checking actual taxable income.
**Solution:**
- Download your ATO notices of assessment early
- Verify you meet the threshold for all 3 years
- If borderline, consider working additional time before applying

### Mistake 2: Insufficient Regional Evidence
**Problem:** Poor documentation of regional residence.
**Solution:**
- Keep all lease agreements and utility bills
- Maintain regional address on all official documents
- Take screenshots of location data if relevant

### Mistake 3: Applying Too Early
**Problem:** Not completing the full 3-year requirement.
**Solution:**
- Wait until you have completed 3 full years
- Count from visa grant date, not arrival date
- Include the full financial years for tax purposes

### Mistake 4: Missing Documents
**Problem:** Incomplete application causing delays.
**Solution:**
- Use this checklist thoroughly
- Upload all documents before submitting
- Keep copies of everything

### Mistake 5: Not Checking Visa Conditions
**Problem:** Unknowingly breaching provisional visa conditions.
**Solution:**
- Review your 491/494 visa conditions regularly
- Ensure you work in nominated occupation (494 holders)
- Live in designated regional area
