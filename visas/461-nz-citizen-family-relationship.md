# Subclass 461 - New Zealand Citizen Family Relationship Visa

> **VISA STATUS:** CURRENTLY OPEN
> > **Last Updated:** March 2025

---

## 1. Executive Overview

The **New Zealand Citizen Family Relationship (Temporary) visa (subclass 461)** allows a person who is **not a New Zealand citizen** but is a member of the family unit of a New Zealand citizen to live and work in Australia for up to 5 years.

### Key Features
- **Duration:** Up to 5 years from date of grant
- **Work Rights:** Full unrestricted work rights
- **Study Rights:** Full study rights
- **Travel:** Unlimited travel in and out of Australia
- **Renewable:** Can apply for subsequent 461 visas
- **Pathway to PR:** Can lead to permanent residency through other pathways

### Who This Visa Is For
- Partners (married or de facto) of NZ citizens
- Dependent children of NZ citizens
- Other eligible family members

### Important Note
The NZ citizen family member must:
- Hold a Special Category visa (subclass 444), OR
- Be travelling to Australia with the applicant
- Not be an "eligible NZ citizen" (protected SCV holder)

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### For the Applicant

#### Basic Requirements
- **Not a New Zealand citizen**
- **Family Relationship:** Must be a member of the family unit of the NZ citizen
- **Health & Character:** Meet standard requirements
- **Health Insurance:** Must maintain adequate health insurance
- **Previous Visa:** Must not have held a 461 visa in the past 5 years (some exceptions apply)

#### Eligible Family Relationships
- **Partner:**
  - Spouse (married)
  - De facto partner (including same-sex)
  - Engaged (intended marriage)
  
- **Dependent Child:**
  - Under 18, or
  - 18-23 and dependent, or
  - 23+ and dependent due to disability

#### Partner Relationship Requirements
- Relationship must be **genuine and continuing**
- Must live together or not apart permanently
- De facto relationships: Generally 12 months together (some exceptions)

### For the NZ Citizen Family Member

#### Requirements
- Must be a **New Zealand citizen**
- Must hold or be eligible for **Special Category visa (subclass 444)**
- Must **NOT** be an "eligible New Zealand citizen" (protected SCV holder)
- Must be in Australia or travelling with applicant

#### "Settled" Requirement
- Generally should be "settled" in Australia
- Evidence may include employment, residence, community ties

### Health Insurance Requirement
- Must maintain **adequate health insurance** (OVHC or Medicare if eligible)
- Evidence required at application and throughout visa period

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Verify NZ Citizen Status
- Confirm NZ citizen holds 444 visa (check VEVO)
- Verify they are NOT a protected SCV holder
- Gather evidence of their settled status

### Step 2: Gather Relationship Evidence
- Collect evidence of genuine relationship
- Document 12+ months de facto (if applicable)
- Prepare Form 888s from friends/family

### Step 3: Health and Character
- Complete health examinations
- Obtain police clearances from all relevant countries
- Arrange health insurance

### Step 4: Prepare Documents
- Identity documents
- Relationship evidence
- NZ citizen's documents
- Health insurance evidence

### Step 5: Lodge Application
- Create or log into ImmiAccount
- Complete online application
- Pay visa fee
- Upload all documents

### Step 6: Wait for Decision
- Processing times vary
- May be granted bridging visa if applied onshore
- Receive visa grant notification

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes
1. **NZ citizen is protected SCV holder** - These citizens cannot sponsor 461
2. **Insufficient relationship evidence** - Must prove genuine and continuing relationship
3. **Missing de facto evidence** - 12 months cohabitation generally required
4. **Inadequate health insurance** - Must maintain for entire stay
5. **Held 461 in past 5 years** - Generally cannot reapply within 5 years
6. **Wrong Form 147 version** - Use current version from DHA website

### Tips for Success
1. **Verify NZ citizen status first** - Check VEVO for 444 visa
2. **Document relationship thoroughly** - More evidence is better
3. **Use Form 888** - Get statutory declarations from Australian citizens/PRs
4. **Maintain health insurance** - Arrange before arrival
5. **Include NZ citizen's settled evidence** - Employment, residence ties
6. **Consider onshore application** - May receive bridging visa

### Important Conditions
- **Condition 8501:** Must maintain health insurance
- **Condition 8558:** Non-resident (cannot establish ongoing residence)
- Work and study permitted without restrictions

### Renewal Information
- Can apply for subsequent 461 visa after expiry
- Must still meet all eligibility requirements
- Each visa valid up to 5 years
- No limit on number of 461 visas
