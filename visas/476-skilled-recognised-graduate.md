# Subclass 476 - Skilled Recognised Graduate Visa

> **VISA STATUS:** PERMANENTLY CLOSED (1 July 2024)
> 
> **Last Updated:** March 2025

---

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Skilled — Recognised Graduate visa (subclass 476)** was a temporary visa designed specifically for recent engineering graduates from recognised institutions worldwide. It allowed graduates to live, work, and study in Australia for up to 18 months to gain valuable professional experience.

### Closure Notice
- **Cap placed:** 22 December 2023
- **Permanently closed:** 1 July 2024
- **No new applications accepted after:** 1 July 2024

### Key Features (Historical)
- **Duration:** 18 months from date of first entry
- **Work Rights:** Full unrestricted work rights
- **Study Rights:** Permitted to study
- **Pathway:** Could lead to skilled migration pathways (482, 186, 189, 190)
- **Travel:** Multiple entries permitted

### Eligible Engineering Disciplines
- Civil Engineering
- Structural Engineering
- Chemical Engineering
- Environmental Engineering
- Electrical and Electronics Engineering
- Mechanical, Production and Plant Engineering
- Mining and Material Engineering

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements (Historical)

### Core Requirements
1. **Age:** Under 31 years at time of application
2. **Qualification:** Completed eligible engineering qualification within past 2 years
3. **Institution:** Degree from specified/recognised institution
4. **First Time:** Must not have previously held a Subclass 476 or 485 visa as primary applicant
5. **English:** Competent English proficiency
6. **Health & Character:** Meet standard requirements

### Recognised Institutions
Qualifications were accepted from:
- **Washington Accord accredited institutions** - Any engineering degree from institutions offering Washington Accord accredited qualifications
- **Specified institutions** listed in legislative instruments

### Engineering Qualifications Accepted
- Bachelor degree
- Master degree
- Doctoral degree
- Postgraduate diploma

### English Language Requirements
- IELTS: Overall 6.0 (minimum 5.0 each band)
- PTE Academic: Overall 50 (minimum 36 each band)
- TOEFL iBT: Overall 64 (minimum 4 each band)
- Cambridge C1 Advanced: Overall 169 (minimum 154 each band)
- Passport from UK, USA, Canada, NZ, or Ireland (exempt)

## 3. Step-by-Step Process (Historical Reference)

### Step 1: Verify Eligibility
- Confirm engineering qualification is from recognised institution
- Check Washington Accord accreditation status
- Ensure degree completed within last 2 years

### Step 2: Prepare Documents
- Gather academic transcripts and completion certificates
- Obtain skills assessment (if applicable)
- Complete English language testing

### Step 3: Health Examinations
- Book medical examination with Bupa Medical Visa Services
- Complete health checks before or after applying

### Step 4: Character Checks
- Obtain police clearances from all countries lived in for 12+ months in past 10 years
- Australian Federal Police check (if applicable)

### Step 5: Lodge Application
- Create ImmiAccount
- Complete online application form
- Pay visa application charge
- Upload all supporting documents

### Step 6: Visa Decision
- Wait for processing
- If granted, enter Australia within specified timeframe
- Visa valid for 18 months from first entry

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Historical Mistakes:
1. **Age miscalculation** - Must be under 31, not 31 or under
2. **Qualification timing** - Degree must be completed within 2 years prior to application
3. **Wrong institution** - Only Washington Accord or specified institutions accepted
4. **Incorrect engineering field** - Non-engineering degrees not accepted
5. **Previous visa holder** - Could not apply if previously held 476 or 485 as primary

### Tips for Alternative Pathways (Current Options):
Since the 476 is closed, engineering graduates should consider:

1. **Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485)**
   - Post-Study Work stream for recent graduates
   - Requires Australian qualification (not available to offshore graduates)

2. **Skilled Independent (Subclass 189)**
   - Points-tested permanent visa
   - Engineering occupations on Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL)

3. **Skilled Nominated (Subclass 190)**
   - State/territory nominated permanent visa
   - Engineering occupations in demand across states

4. **Skilled Work Regional (Subclass 491)**
   - Provisional visa with pathway to PR
   - Regional areas often have engineering shortages

5. **Employer Sponsored (Subclass 482)**
   - Requires 2 years experience (can gain overseas first)

## ALTERNATIVE PATHWAYS FOR ENGINEERING GRADUATES

Since the Subclass 476 is closed, engineering graduates should consider:

### For Offshore Graduates (No Australian Study):
1. **Skilled Migration (189/190/491)**
   - Requires skills assessment through Engineers Australia
   - Need 65+ points
   - Engineering occupations generally on MLTSSL

2. **Employer Sponsorship (482/186)**
   - Requires 2 years post-qualification experience
   - Find employer willing to sponsor

### For Onshore Graduates:
1. **Subclass 485 (Temporary Graduate)**
   - Available if studied in Australia
   - 2-4 years depending on qualification
