# Subclass 444 - Special Category Visa (NZ Citizens)

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Special Category Visa (Subclass 444)** is a temporary visa granted to New Zealand citizens upon arrival in Australia. This visa is granted automatically at the border under the Trans-Tasman Travel Arrangement and allows NZ citizens to live, work, and study in Australia indefinitely while they remain NZ citizens.

**Key Features:**
- **No Application Required** - Granted automatically on arrival
- **Duration:** Valid for duration of stay (ceases upon departure)
- **Work Rights:** Full unlimited work rights
- **Study Rights:** Full study rights
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Renewal:** New visa granted each time you enter Australia

**Important Note:** From 1 July 2023, changes to citizenship eligibility for NZ citizens holding a non-protected SCV came into effect.

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### Primary Requirements:
- **Citizenship:** Must be a New Zealand citizen
- **Travel Document:** Valid New Zealand passport
- **Character:** Must meet character requirements
- **Health:** Must meet health requirements (may be checked at border)
- **Intent:** Genuine intention to visit/stay in Australia

### On Arrival Requirements:
- Complete incoming passenger card
- Present valid NZ passport
- Declare any items of quarantine concern
- Answer health and character questions honestly

### Protected vs Non-Protected SCV:

**Protected SCV (Generally pre-2001 arrivals):**
- Eligible for certain social security payments
- Different citizenship pathway

**Non-Protected SCV (Post-2001 arrivals):**
- From 1 July 2023: Can apply for citizenship by conferral after meeting residence requirements
- Not eligible for most social security payments
- Must apply for permanent residency through standard pathways

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Prepare Documentation
- Ensure NZ passport is valid
- Check passport has at least 6 months validity
- Prepare travel itinerary if questioned

### Step 2: Travel to Australia
- Book travel to Australia
- Complete incoming passenger card on arrival
- Proceed to passport control

### Step 3: Border Processing
- Present NZ passport to border officer
- Answer standard entry questions
- Visa granted electronically (no stamp usually)

### Step 4: Verify Visa Grant
- Check visa status via VEVO (Visa Entitlement Verification Online)
- Link passport to ImmiAccount if desired

### For Children Born in Australia to NZ Citizen Parents:
- Child is not automatically Australian citizen
- Must apply for NZ citizenship by descent
- Then obtain NZ passport
- Apply for SCV (Form 444) or use that passport to enter Australia

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes:
1. **Expired Passport** - Ensure NZ passport is valid for intended stay
2. **Character Issues** - Criminal history may affect entry
3. **Health Concerns** - Serious health issues may require assessment
4. **Confusion with Permanent Residency** - SCV is temporary, not permanent
5. **Not Understanding SCV Type** - Know if you hold protected or non-protected SCV
6. **Children's Status** - Children born in Australia to SCV holders are not Australian citizens

### Pro Tips:
1. **Check VEVO Regularly** - Verify your visa status online
2. **Maintain NZ Citizenship** - SCV ceases if you lose NZ citizenship
3. **Understand Your Rights** - Know the difference between protected and non-protected SCV
4. **Citizenship Pathway** - From July 2023, non-protected SCV holders can apply for citizenship
5. **Keep Records** - Maintain evidence of your residence for citizenship applications
6. **Health Insurance** - Consider private health insurance as no automatic Medicare access

### 10F. Citizenship Pathway (Post-July 2023)

For non-protected SCV holders:
- Must reside in Australia for 4 years
- Must hold valid visa for 12 months prior to application
- Must meet character requirements
- Processing time for citizenship: 12-24 months

### 10H. Protected vs Non-Protected SCV Check

**You likely have PROTECTED SCV if:**
- You were in Australia on 26 February 2001 as an SCV holder
- You were outside Australia on 26 February 2001 but in Australia for 12 months in the 2 years before that date

**You have NON-PROTECTED SCV if:**
- You arrived in Australia after 26 February 2001
- You don't meet the above criteria
