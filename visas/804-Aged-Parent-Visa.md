# Subclass 804 - Aged Parent Visa (Onshore)

## Section 1: Executive Overview

The Aged Parent visa (subclass 804) is an onshore permanent visa for older parents of Australian citizens, permanent residents, or eligible New Zealand citizens. This visa allows parents to remain in Australia while their application is processed, though processing times are extremely long.

**Key Features:**
- **Visa Type:** Permanent Resident
- **Application Location:** Must be in Australia to apply
- **Age Requirement:** Must be old enough to receive Australian Age Pension
- **Cost:** Lower than contributory visas (~AUD $7,000 total)
- **Processing Time:** 13-29+ years (currently queued at mid-2013)
- **Bridging Visa:** Granted upon application, allowing stay in Australia

**Age Pension Qualifying Age:**
| Birth Date | Qualifying Age |
|------------|----------------|
| Before July 1, 1952 | 65 years |
| July 1952 - Dec 1953 | 65.5 years |
| Jan 1954 - June 1955 | 66 years |
| July 1955 - Dec 1956 | 66.5 years |
| From Jan 1, 1957 | 67 years |

---

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements

### Age Requirement

Applicants must be old enough to qualify for the Australian Age Pension:
- Currently 67 years for those born from January 1, 1957
- See table above for earlier birth dates

### Balance of Family Test

Must meet the same test as Subclass 103:
- At least half of children live permanently in Australia, OR
- More children in Australia than any other single country

### Sponsor Requirements

Sponsor must be:
- Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- Settled in Australia (usually 2+ years)
- At least 18 years old
- Child of applicant (or child's spouse/partner)

### Location Requirements

- Must be in Australia when applying
- Must be in Australia when visa is decided
- Cannot hold or have held a Sponsored Parent (870) visa

### Health and Character

- Meet health requirements
- Meet character requirements
- Assurance of Support may be required

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Verify Age Qualification
- Check qualifying age based on birth date
- Must have reached qualifying age

### Step 2: Check Balance of Family Test
- Document children's locations
- Confirm at least half are in Australia

### Step 3: Enter Australia
- Must be in Australia on valid visa to apply
- Common pathways: Visitor visa, ETA, eVisitor

### Step 4: Gather Documents
- Identity documents
- Age evidence (birth certificate, passport)
- Family relationship evidence
- Children's status evidence

### Step 5: Create ImmiAccount
- Register at https://online.immi.gov.au

### Step 6: Complete Application
- Select "Parent" → "Aged Parent (804)"
- Answer all questions
- Upload documents

### Step 7: Pay First Instalment
- Base application charge
- Additional applicant charges

### Step 8: Submit Application
- Receive Bridging Visa A automatically
- Can remain in Australia
- Receive queue date

### Step 9: Wait in Queue
- Current queue: Mid-2013 applications
- Estimated wait: 13-29+ years

### Step 10: Request for Final Processing
- When queue date reached:
  - Second visa charge required
  - Health examinations
  - Police certificates
  - Assurance of Support

### Step 11: Visa Grant
- Permanent residency granted

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes

1. **Not Meeting Age Requirement**
   - Mistake: Applying before qualifying age
   - Solution: Wait until Age Pension qualifying age

2. **No Entry Visa**
   - Mistake: Trying to apply without valid Australian visa
   - Solution: Must enter Australia on valid visa first

3. **Condition 8503 (No Further Stay)**
   - Mistake: Holding visa with No Further Stay condition
   - Solution: Apply for waiver or wait for condition to expire

4. **Underestimating Wait Time**
   - Mistake: Not realizing 15+ year processing
   - Solution: Consider Contributory Aged Parent (864) visa

### Expert Tips

**Before Applying:**
- Enter Australia on long-validity visitor visa
- Ensure no "No Further Stay" condition
- Seriously consider Contributory option (864)
- Plan for long wait with Bridging Visa

**While on Bridging Visa:**
- Register for Medicare
- Apply for work rights if needed (Form 1005)
- Can apply for BVB for overseas travel
- Keep contact details updated

**When Queue Date Reached:**
- Prepare Assurance of Support early
- Complete health examinations promptly
- Ensure sponsor still eligible

**Queue Progression:**
- Queue advances approximately 1 year per year
- Onshore and offshore parent visas share processing
- Limited annual places create long waits

**Advantages of Onshore (804):**
- Can stay in Australia on Bridging Visa
- Medicare access
- Can apply for work rights
- No need to leave Australia

### 10D. Current Trends (2024-2025)

**Processing:**
- Queue at mid-2013
- No acceleration expected
- Government reviewing parent visa system

**Bridging Visa Considerations:**
- Most applicants remain on BVA for 10+ years
- Work rights restrictions apply
- Travel restrictions (need BVB for overseas travel)
- Medicare available

### 10E. Year-by-Year Expectations

**Year 1:**

**Years 2-15 (Queue Period):**

**Years 15+ (Approaching Queue Date):**

- Arrange Assurance of Support

3. **Medicare:**
   - Register after BVA granted

### 10G. Alternatives During Wait

**If Wait Becomes Unacceptable:**

1. **Switch to Contributory (864):**
   - Can apply while in Australia
   - Higher cost but faster (5-7 years)
   - No need to leave Australia

2. **Temporary Parent Visa (870):**
   - Cannot hold while 804 is processing
   - Would need to withdraw 804 first

3. **Visitor Visas:**
   - Continue on bridging visa
   - Or apply for new visitor visa if 804 withdrawn

### 10H. Bridging Visa Considerations

**Bridging Visa A (Automatic):**
- Granted when valid visa expires
- Allows stay until 804 decision
- Work rights: Apply separately (Form 1005)
- Travel: Apply for BVB to leave Australia

**Important:**
- Cannot re-enter on BVA alone
- Must apply for BVB before travel
- BVB has limited validity
