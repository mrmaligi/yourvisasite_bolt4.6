# Subclass 476 - Skilled Recognised Graduate Visa

## 1. Executive Overview

**IMPORTANT: This visa is CLOSED to new applications as of 1 July 2024.**

The **Skilled Recognised Graduate Visa (Subclass 476)** was a temporary visa for recent engineering graduates from recognised institutions. It allowed graduates to live, work, and study in Australia for up to 18 months to gain skilled work experience.

**Status:** 
- Cap placed on visa: 22 December 2023
- **Closed permanently: 1 July 2024**
- No new applications accepted

**Historical Key Features:**
- Duration: Up to 18 months
- Work Rights: Full work rights
- Study Rights: Permitted
- Cost: AUD $405 (when open)
- Target Group: Engineering graduates from specified institutions

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements (Historical)

### Requirements When Visa Was Open:

#### Primary Requirements:
- **Age:** Under 31 years at time of application
- **Qualification:** Engineering degree (bachelor, master, doctoral, or postgraduate diploma)
- **Institution:** From a specified recognised institution
- **Timing:** Within 2 years of completing degree
- **English:** Competent English (IELTS 6.0 overall or equivalent)
- **Health & Character:** Must meet requirements

#### Recognised Engineering Disciplines:
- Civil Engineering
- Structural Engineering
- Chemical Engineering
- Environmental Engineering
- Electrical and Electronics Engineering
- Mechanical, Production and Plant Engineering
- Mining and Material Engineering

#### Recognised Institutions (included):
- Washington Accord institutions
- Specific institutions from various countries including UK, USA, Canada, NZ, and others

## 3. Step-by-Step Process (Historical)

### When Visa Was Open:

1. **Complete Engineering Degree** - From recognised institution
2. **Within 2 Years** - Apply within 2 years of graduation
3. **Check Age** - Ensure under 31 years
4. **English Test** - Obtain IELTS 6.0 or equivalent
5. **Gather Documents** - Passport, qualifications, English results
6. **Apply Online** - Via ImmiAccount
7. **Health Checks** - Complete medical examinations
8. **Wait for Decision** - Processing typically 3-6 months

## 10. ALTERNATIVE PATHWAYS FOR ENGINEERING GRADUATES

### 10A. Current Options for Engineering Graduates

Since the closure of Subclass 476, engineering graduates should consider:

#### Option 1: Temporary Graduate Visa (Subclass 485)
- For graduates of Australian institutions
- Up to 2-4 years depending on qualification
- Work rights included
- Pathway to permanent residency

#### Option 2: Temporary Skill Shortage Visa (Subclass 482)
- Employer-sponsored pathway
- Requires skilled occupation list nomination
- Engineering occupations eligible
- Pathway to PR after 2 years

#### Option 3: Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)
- Points-tested visa
- Engineering occupations on Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List (MLTSSL)
- Permanent residency
- Competitive points threshold

#### Option 4: Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)
- State/territory nominated
- Additional 5 points for nomination
- Permanent residency
- State-specific occupation lists

#### Option 5: Skilled Work Regional Visa (Subclass 491)
- Regional pathway
- 15 points for regional nomination
- Provisional visa leading to PR (subclass 191)
- Regional occupation lists

### 10B. Skills Assessment for Engineers

Engineers Australia is the assessing authority:
- **Competency Demonstration Report (CDR)** - For non-accredited qualifications
- **Accredited Qualifications** - Streamlined assessment
- **Processing Time:** 8-12 weeks
- **Cost:** $800 - $1,200 AUD

### 10C. Engineering Occupations on Skilled Lists

Common engineering occupations:
- Civil Engineer
- Mechanical Engineer
- Electrical Engineer
- Chemical Engineer
- Mining Engineer
- Petroleum Engineer
- Structural Engineer
- Transport Engineer

### 10D. Tips for Engineering Graduates

1. **Get Skills Assessment Early** - Required for skilled visas
2. **Gain Work Experience** - Essential for points and employer sponsorship
3. **Improve English Score** - Higher scores = more points
4. **Consider State Nomination** - Additional points and priority processing
5. **Regional Options** - More opportunities in regional areas
6. **Professional Year** - Can add points and improve employability

## 11. Summary

The Subclass 476 visa was a valuable pathway for engineering graduates but has been permanently closed. Graduates should now pursue alternative pathways through skilled migration, employer sponsorship, or the Temporary Graduate visa (if studying in Australia).

For current options, consult a registered migration agent or check the Department of Home Affairs website for the most up-to-date visa options.
