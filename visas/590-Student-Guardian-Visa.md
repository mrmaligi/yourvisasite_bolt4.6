# Subclass 590 - Student Guardian Visa

## Section 1: Executive Overview

**Visa Type:** Temporary Guardian Visa  
**Purpose:** Allows parents/guardians to accompany and care for student visa holders under 18  
**Duration:** Up to 5 years (matches student's visa)  
**Cost:** AUD $710 (base application charge)  
**Processing Time:** 4-6 months (90% of applications)

### What is the Subclass 590 Visa?

The Subclass 590 Student Guardian Visa is a **temporary visa** designed for parents or legal guardians of international students studying in Australia on a Subclass 500 Student Visa. This visa allows the guardian to live in Australia to **provide care, welfare, and support** for a student who is under 18 years of age (or over 18 in exceptional circumstances).

### Key Benefits

- **Care for your child** - Live in Australia while they study
- **Match student visa duration** - Valid for same period as student's visa
- **Multiple entries** - Travel to and from Australia
- **Study limited** - Can study English for less than 20 hours per week
- **Healthcare access** - Must maintain adequate health insurance
- **No work rights** - Cannot work while on this visa

### Important Limitations

- **No work permitted** - Cannot engage in paid employment
- **Limited study** - Only English language study (max 20 hours/week)
- **Guardian obligations** - Must live with and care for the student
- **Accommodation requirement** - Must arrange accommodation
- **Welfare responsibility** - Accountable for student's welfare

### Who Is This Visa For?

- Parents of international students under 18
- Legal guardians of student visa holders
- Relatives who have been nominated as custodians
- Those who can demonstrate genuine temporary stay

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements

### Core Eligibility Criteria

#### 1. Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE)
- Must demonstrate **genuine intention** to stay temporarily
- Must show ties to home country
- Must have compelling reasons to return home after visa expires
- Cannot use this as a pathway to permanent residency

#### 2. Relationship to Student
- Must be the **parent, legal guardian, or nominated relative** of the student
- Must be nominated by the student's parent or legal guardian
- Must be approved as the student's welfare provider by the education provider

#### 3. Student Requirements
- The student must:
  - Hold a Subclass 500 Student Visa
  - Be under 18 years of age (or over 18 with exceptional circumstances)
  - Be enrolled in a registered course
  - Have arranged welfare if under 18

#### 4. Welfare Arrangements
- Must be approved as the student's **welfare provider**
- Must live with the student during their stay
- Must maintain adequate arrangements for accommodation
- Must maintain adequate arrangements for support

#### 5. Financial Capacity
- Must demonstrate **sufficient funds** to support yourself and the student
- Must show funds for:
  - Living expenses (guardian): AUD $21,041 per year
  - Living expenses (student): AUD $21,041 per year
  - Course fees (if applicable)
  - Travel costs
- Can use: personal funds, partner's income, or sponsorship

#### 6. Health Insurance
- Must maintain **adequate health insurance** for entire stay
- Must arrange Overseas Visitor Health Cover (OVHC)
- Insurance must cover the student if not covered by OSHC

### Additional Requirements

#### Health Requirements
- Must meet Australia's health standards
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Must not pose a public health risk

#### Character Requirements
- Must meet Australia's character requirements
- Provide police clearances if requested
- Declare any criminal history

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Confirm Eligibility (Week 1)
- Ensure student has valid Subclass 500 visa
- Confirm your relationship to the student
- Check student is under 18 (or exceptional circumstances apply)
- Verify education provider approves you as welfare provider

### Step 2: Arrange Health Insurance (Week 1)
- Purchase Overseas Visitor Health Cover (OVHC)
- Ensure coverage meets DHA requirements
- Ensure coverage period matches intended stay

### Step 3: Prepare Financial Evidence (Weeks 1-2)
- Gather bank statements (3-6 months)
- Prepare evidence of income (if applicable)
- Calculate required funds:
  - Guardian living: $21,041/year
  - Student living: $21,041/year
  - Tuition fees (remaining)
  - Travel costs: $2,000+ per person

### Step 4: Obtain Welfare Approval (Week 2)
- Contact education provider
- Submit welfare arrangement forms
- Obtain written approval as welfare provider
- Keep approval documentation

### Step 5: Create ImmiAccount (Week 2)
- Create or access ImmiAccount
- Prepare for online application

### Step 6: Lodge Application (Week 3)
- Complete online application form
- Pay visa application charge (AUD $710)
- Upload all supporting documents
- Submit application

### Step 7: Health Examinations (Weeks 3-6)
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Book through Bupa Medical Visa Services
- Results valid for 12 months

### Step 8: Wait for Processing (Months 2-6)
- Monitor ImmiAccount for updates
- Processing typically 4-6 months
- Apply at least 6 weeks before intended travel

### Step 9: Visa Grant
- Receive grant notification
- Check visa conditions carefully
- Make travel arrangements

## Section 7: Key Considerations

### Visa Conditions

The Subclass 590 visa has strict conditions:
- **Condition 8101:** No work
- **Condition 8104:** Study limited to less than 3 months (English only)
- **Condition 8501:** Maintain adequate health insurance
- **Condition 8534:** No further stay (cannot apply for most other visas in Australia)
- **Condition 8537:** Maintain welfare arrangements for student

### No Further Stay Condition (8534)

**Important:** The 590 visa typically includes a "No Further Stay" condition, which means:
- Cannot apply for most other visas while in Australia
- Must leave Australia when visa expires
- Cannot extend stay by applying for another visa onshore
- Very limited exceptions apply (compassionate/compelling circumstances)

### Welfare Obligations

As a guardian, you must:
- Live with the student
- Provide appropriate accommodation
- Provide care and support
- Ensure student attends school
- Notify education provider of any changes
- Maintain regular contact with education provider

### Changing Circumstances

If circumstances change:
- Student turns 18 - may need to leave or change arrangements
- Student changes courses - notify DHA
- Relationship changes - notify DHA
- Financial situation changes - notify DHA

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Insufficient Financial Evidence
**Problem:** Not showing enough funds for entire stay.
**Solution:**
- Calculate full costs for visa duration
- Show funds for both guardian and student
- Include course fees, living costs, travel
- Use bank statements showing consistent funds

### Mistake 2: Missing Welfare Approval
**Problem:** Education provider hasn't approved welfare arrangements.
**Solution:**
- Contact education provider early
- Complete all required forms
- Obtain written approval before applying
- Submit approval with visa application

### Mistake 3: Weak GTE Evidence
**Problem:** Not demonstrating intention to return home.
**Solution:**
- Show strong ties to home country
- Explain what you'll return to
- Provide property, employment, family evidence
- Write clear GTE statement

### Mistake 4: Inadequate Health Insurance
**Problem:** Wrong type or insufficient coverage.
**Solution:**
- Purchase Overseas Visitor Health Cover (OVHC)
- Ensure it meets DHA requirements
- Coverage must be for entire stay
- Include confirmation in application

### Mistake 5: Applying Too Late
**Problem:** Not allowing enough processing time.
**Solution:**
- Apply at least 6-8 weeks before travel
- Processing can take up to 6 months
- Don't book flights until visa granted
- Start process early
