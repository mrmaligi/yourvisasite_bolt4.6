# Subclass 101 - Child Visa (Offshore)

## Section 1: Executive Overview

**Visa Type:** Permanent Resident Visa  
**Purpose:** Allows children outside Australia to live permanently with their parents in Australia  
**Duration:** Permanent - indefinite stay  
**Cost:** AUD $3,055 (base application charge)  
**Processing Time:** 18-27 months (90% of applications)

### What is the Subclass 101 Child Visa?

The Subclass 101 Child Visa is a **permanent residency visa** designed for children of Australian citizens, permanent residents, or eligible New Zealand citizens who are living **outside Australia**. This visa allows the child to migrate to Australia to join their parent(s) and live permanently.

### Key Benefits

- **Permanent residency** - Live in Australia indefinitely
- **Medicare access** - Immediate enrollment in public healthcare
- **Work rights** - Can work when of legal age
- **Study rights** - Access to education at domestic rates
- **Citizenship pathway** - Eligible for citizenship after meeting requirements
- **Sponsor family** - Can sponsor eligible relatives in future
- **Travel flexibility** - Travel to and from Australia for 5 years from grant

### Who Is This Visa For?

- Biological children of Australian citizens/permanent residents
- Adopted children (with legal documentation)
- Step-children (in certain circumstances)
- Children under 25 years of age
- Children who are dependent on their Australian parent

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements

### Core Eligibility Criteria

#### 1. Relationship to Sponsor
Must be the **child** of an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen:
- Biological child
- Adopted child (legal adoption)
- Step-child (if under 18 and parent is partner of sponsor)

#### 2. Age Requirements
Must be:
- **Under 18 years** of age, OR
- **18-25 years** and a full-time student, OR
- **18+ years** and unable to work due to disability

#### 3. Marital Status
- Must be **single** (not married, engaged, or in de facto relationship)
- Must not have a partner

#### 4. Location
- Must be **outside Australia** when applying
- Must be outside Australia when visa is granted
- This is an **offshore** visa

#### 5. Sponsorship
- Must be **sponsored by parent** who is Australian citizen/permanent resident
- Sponsor must be 18 years or older
- If under 18, sponsor must be relative, guardian, or community organization

#### 6. Health and Character
- Must meet Australia's health standards
- Must meet character requirements (if 16 years or older)
- Must not have outstanding debts to Australian government

### Special Circumstances

#### Adopted Children
- Adoption must be legal and recognized
- Must have been adopted before turning 18
- Different rules apply for Hague Adoption and non-Hague Adoption

#### Step-Children
- Parent must be partner of sponsor
- Child must be under 18
- Relationship must be genuine and continuing

#### Orphan Relative
- If child's parents are deceased, unable to care for them, or cannot be found
- May be eligible for Orphan Relative visa (subclass 117) instead

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Confirm Eligibility (Week 1)
- Verify parent is Australian citizen/permanent resident
- Confirm child meets age requirements
- Check child is single
- Confirm child is outside Australia
- Gather birth/adoption documents

### Step 2: Obtain Parent Documents (Weeks 1-2)
- Parent's Australian passport or citizenship certificate
- Or parent's permanent resident visa evidence
- Evidence of relationship to child (birth certificate)
- Parent's ID documents

### Step 3: Prepare Child Documents (Weeks 1-2)
- Child's passport
- Child's birth certificate
- Evidence of single status (if over 18)
- Evidence of student status (if 18-25 and studying)
- Medical/disability evidence (if applicable)

### Step 4: Arrange Health Examinations (Week 2)
- Book health examinations through Bupa
- Child must complete medical assessment
- Results valid for 12 months

### Step 5: Create ImmiAccount (Week 2)
- Create or access ImmiAccount
- Parent will be the sponsor
- Prepare for online application

### Step 6: Lodge Application (Week 3)
- Complete online application form
- Pay visa application charge (AUD $3,055)
- Upload all supporting documents
- Submit application from outside Australia

### Step 7: Wait for Processing (Months 6-27)
- Processing is lengthy: 18-27 months
- Monitor ImmiAccount for updates
- Respond promptly to any requests
- Keep health examinations current (renew if expire)

### Step 8: Visa Grant
- Receive grant notification
- Child can travel to Australia
- Visa activated on first entry
- Medicare enrollment can begin

## Section 4: Document Checklist

### Child's Identity Documents
- [ ] Current passport (bio page)
- [ ] Birth certificate
- [ ] Recent passport-sized photographs
- [ ] Name change documents (if applicable)

### Parent's Identity Documents
- [ ] Australian passport (bio page), OR
- [ ] Australian citizenship certificate, OR
- [ ] Permanent resident visa grant notice
- [ ] Driver's license or other ID

### Relationship Evidence (CRITICAL)
- [ ] **Child's birth certificate showing parent's name**
- [ ] If adopted: Adoption certificate and legal documents
- [ ] If step-child: Marriage certificate showing parent's relationship to sponsor
- [ ] If parent's name changed: Name change documents linking to birth certificate
- [ ] Family book/household register (if applicable)
- [ ] Photos of parent and child together
- [ ] Evidence of ongoing relationship (communications, financial support)

### Age/Status Evidence
- [ ] Evidence of single status (if over 18):
  - Statutory declaration
  - No marriage certificate
- [ ] Evidence of full-time study (if 18-25):
  - School enrollment letter
  - Student ID card
  - Academic transcripts
- [ ] Evidence of disability (if 18+ and unable to work):
  - Medical reports
  - Disability assessment

### Health Documents
- [ ] Health examination results (Bupa)
- [ ] Medical reports (if child has medical conditions)
- [ ] Vaccination records

### Character Documents (if child 16+)
- [ ] Police clearances from countries lived in 12+ months (last 10 years)
- [ ] Australian Federal Police check (if applicable)
- [ ] Court documents (if applicable)

### Sponsor Documents
- [ ] Sponsor declaration (Form 40CH)
- [ ] Evidence of settled status in Australia
- [ ] Evidence of accommodation for child
- [ ] Evidence of financial capacity to support child
- [ ] Employment letter or tax returns

### Form 47CH (if applicable)
- [ ] Completed by other parent if not migrating
- [ ] Consent for child to migrate
- [ ] Legal custody documents (if applicable)

## Section 7: Key Considerations

### Onshore vs Offshore

**Subclass 101 (Offshore):**
- Must be outside Australia when applying and granted
- Longer processing times
- No bridging visa

**Subclass 802 (Onshore):**
- Must be in Australia when applying
- Similar processing times
- May be eligible for bridging visa

### Best Interests of the Child

DHA must consider the **best interests of the child**:
- Relationship with parent in Australia
- Child's welfare and development
- Family unity
- Cultural and community ties

### Custody Considerations

If parents are separated:
- Consent may be required from both parents
- Custody arrangements must be clear
- Legal documentation may be required
- DHA will assess custody situation carefully

### Health Issues

If child has health conditions:
- Must meet health requirement
- Significant cost to Australian community may affect outcome
- Can seek health waiver in some circumstances
- Medical bond may be required

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Insufficient Relationship Evidence
**Problem:** Not proving genuine parent-child relationship.
**Solution:**
- Provide full birth certificate showing parent's name
- Include family photos over time
- Show evidence of ongoing relationship
- Include communications records
- Provide financial support evidence

### Mistake 2: Wrong Visa Subclass
**Problem:** Applying for 101 when child is in Australia (should apply for 802).
**Solution:**
- Confirm child's location before applying
- Apply for 802 if child is in Australia
- Apply for 101 if child is outside Australia
- Location at time of application is key

### Mistake 3: Child Turns 18 During Processing
**Problem:** Child ages out of eligibility during long processing.
**Solution:**
- Apply as early as possible
- If 18-25, maintain full-time student status
- If over 18, ensure single status
- Monitor processing times

### Mistake 4: Missing Parental Consent
**Problem:** Not obtaining consent from both parents.
**Solution:**
- Get consent from non-sponsoring parent (Form 47CH)
- Provide custody documents if applicable
- Explain if other parent cannot be located
- Seek legal advice if custody disputes

### Mistake 5: Health Examinations Expire
**Problem:** Health exams expire during long processing.
**Solution:**
- Health exams valid for 12 months
- Monitor expiry dates
- Book new exams before expiry if processing extends
- DHA will request new exams if needed

### 10F. How to Track Your Application

**ImmiAccount Status Meanings:**
- **Received:** Application lodged successfully
- **Initial Assessment:** Document review underway
- **Further Assessment:** Additional checks occurring
- **Finalised:** Decision made

**When to Follow Up:**
- After 24 months if no updates
- If requested information provided and no response in 8 weeks
- Submit enquiry through ImmiAccount

### 10G. Tips to Speed Up Processing

1. **Submit complete application** - All documents with initial lodgement
2. **Clear relationship evidence** - Comprehensive birth/adoption documents
3. **Valid health exams** - Complete before expiry
4. **Respond quickly** - To any requests for additional information
5. **Monitor expiry dates** - Renew health exams if needed
6. **Keep passport valid** - Must be valid at grant

### 10H. If Your Application is Delayed

**When is it considered delayed?**
- Beyond 27 months with no contact
- No response to provided information after 8 weeks

**How to Enquire:**
- Submit enquiry through ImmiAccount
- Include TRN (Transaction Reference Number)
- Contact local MP for assistance
- Seek help from migration agent

**Common Issues:**
- Relationship verification
- Character check delays
- Health assessment queries
- Custody complications
