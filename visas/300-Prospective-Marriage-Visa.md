# Subclass 300 - Prospective Marriage (Fiancé) Visa

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Prospective Marriage Visa (Subclass 300)** is for individuals who want to come to Australia to marry their prospective spouse (an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen). This is a temporary 9-month visa that allows the holder to enter Australia, marry their sponsor, and then apply for a Partner visa (820/801).

**Key Features:**
- Duration: 9 months from date of grant
- Purpose: To marry prospective spouse in Australia
- Work Rights: Full work rights
- Study Rights: Permitted
- Travel Rights: Multiple entries
- Cost: ~AUD $7,000 - $9,365
- Pathway: Apply for 820/801 after marriage (reduced fee)

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### Primary Requirements:
- **Relationship:** Must intend to marry an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- **Age:** Both partners must be 18+ years (limited exceptions)
- **Meeting:** Must have met in person as adults
- **Genuine Relationship:** Genuine intention to marry and live as spouses
- **Health & Character:** Must meet requirements
- **Sponsor:** Prospective spouse must be approved as sponsor
- **Marriage Timeline:** Must marry within 9 months of visa grant

### Sponsor Requirements:
- Must be Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- Must be 18+ years
- Must not be related (by blood or adoption)
- Can only sponsor twice in lifetime (waivers available in compelling circumstances)
- Must meet character requirements
- 5-year wait between sponsorships (unless exceptional circumstances)

### Relationship Requirements:
- Must have met in person
- Must have genuine intention to marry
- Must intend to live together as spouses
- Marriage must be valid under Australian law

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Confirm Eligibility
- Verify you have met in person
- Check sponsor eligibility
- Confirm intention to marry within 9 months
- Gather relationship evidence

### Step 2: Gather Evidence
**Meeting Evidence:**
- Photos together
- Travel documents showing visits
- Evidence of time spent together

**Relationship Evidence:**
- Communication records
- Evidence of ongoing relationship
- Future plans together

**Wedding Plans (if applicable):**
- Wedding bookings (not required but helpful)
- Intended date
- Venue information

### Step 3: Sponsor Application
- Sponsor completes sponsorship form
- Police checks for sponsor
- Evidence of Australian status

### Step 4: Applicant Documents
- Identity documents
- Health examinations
- Police clearances
- Character documents

### Step 5: Lodge Application
- Can lodge onshore or offshore
- Create ImmiAccount
- Complete online application
- Upload all documents
- Pay visa application charge

### Step 6: After Lodgement
- Complete health examinations
- Provide biometrics if requested
- Wait for decision

### Step 7: Visa Grant
- Enter Australia (if applied offshore)
- Must marry within 9 months
- Can work and study immediately

### Step 8: Marriage
- Marry your prospective spouse
- Register marriage in Australia
- Obtain marriage certificate

### Step 9: Apply for Partner Visa (820/801)
- Apply for onshore partner visa
- Reduced fee applies ($1,560)
- Stay in Australia on bridging visa while processing

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes:
1. **Not Met in Person** - Must have physically met as adults
2. **Insufficient Meeting Evidence** - Need proof of time spent together
3. **Missing Sponsor Checks** - Sponsor needs police clearance
4. **Not Marrying in Time** - Must marry within 9 months
5. **Incomplete Relationship Evidence** - Need to show genuine relationship
6. **Waiting Too Long** - Processing can take 15+ months

### Pro Tips:
1. **Plan Ahead** - Processing times are long; plan wedding accordingly
2. **Document Meetings** - Keep evidence of all visits/time together
3. **Sponsor Preparation** - Ensure sponsor meets all requirements
4. **Wedding Flexibility** - Don't book non-refundable wedding until visa granted
5. **Consider Direct Partner Visa** - If already eligible for de facto, may be better option
6. **Keep Communicating** - Document ongoing relationship
7. **Understand 9-Month Limit** - Have contingency plans
8. **Reduced Fee Pathway** - Remember 820/801 fee is reduced after 300
