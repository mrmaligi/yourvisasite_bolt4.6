# Subclass 444 - Special Category Visa

> **VISA STATUS:** CURRENTLY OPEN - AUTOMATIC GRANT ON ARRIVAL
> > **Last Updated:** March 2025

---

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Special Category visa (subclass 444)** is a temporary visa granted automatically to New Zealand citizens upon arrival in Australia. It allows NZ citizens to visit, study, stay, and work in Australia indefinitely as long as they remain NZ citizens.

### Key Features
- **Grant Method:** Automatic on arrival (no application required)
- **Duration:** Valid until departure from Australia
- **Work Rights:** Full unrestricted work rights
- **Study Rights:** Full study rights
- **Cost:** FREE (no visa application charge)
- **Medicare:** Eligible for reciprocal health care (with restrictions)

### Important Distinction
There are two types of SCV:
1. **Protected SCV** - Arrived in Australia before 26 February 2001
2. **Non-protected SCV** - Arrived on or after 26 February 2001

This distinction affects eligibility for Australian citizenship and certain social security benefits.

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements
To be granted a Subclass 444 on arrival:

1. **New Zealand Citizen**
   - Must hold valid New Zealand passport
   - Must be travelling on NZ passport

2. **Health Requirements**
   - Must meet health requirements
   - No tuberculosis
   - No condition posing significant healthcare costs

3. **Character Requirements**
   - Must meet character requirements
   - No substantial criminal record
   - Not of concern to national security

4. **No 'No Further Stay' Condition**
   - Must not have a "no further stay" condition on a previous visa

### Automatic Grant Process
- No prior application required
- Granted electronically on arrival
- Linked to passport
- Can verify status via VEVO

### Step 1: Ensure Eligibility
- Confirm NZ citizenship
- Ensure passport is valid
- Check no health or character concerns

### Step 2: Travel to Australia

- Set up bank account
- Enrol in Medicare (if eligible)
- Apply for driver licence (if needed)

## 8. Common Mistakes > Tips

### Common Issues
1. **Dual citizens using wrong passport** - Must use NZ passport to get 444
2. **Health concerns** - TB or significant health issues may prevent grant
3. **Character issues** - Serious criminal history may affect eligibility
4. **Previous visa conditions** - "No further stay" condition may prevent grant
5. **Children born in Australia** - Different citizenship rules apply

### Tips for Smooth Entry
1. **Always use NZ passport** - Even if dual citizen
2. **Declare health issues** - Honesty prevents future problems
3. **Check criminal history** - Serious offences may affect entry
4. **Keep passport valid** - Cannot renew 444 without valid NZ passport
5. **Understand SCV limitations** - Not permanent residency

### Protected vs Non-Protected SCV

#### Protected SCV (Arrived before 26 February 2001)
- Eligible for Australian citizenship (by conferral)
- Eligible for certain social security payments
- Can sponsor family members for some visas
- Treated similarly to permanent residents for some purposes

#### Non-Protected SCV (Arrived on/after 26 February 2001)
- **From 1 July 2023:** Eligible to apply for citizenship by conferral
- Must meet 4-year residence requirement
- Must meet character requirements
- Must pass citizenship test
- Limited social security access

### Citizenship Pathway (Non-Protected SCV)
From 1 July 2023, non-protected SCV holders can apply for citizenship:
- Must have held SCV for 4 years
- Must have been physically present in Australia for specified periods
- Must pass citizenship test
- Must meet character requirements

## RIGHTS AND ENTITLEMENTS

### Work Rights
- **Unrestricted** - Can work for any employer
- Full-time, part-time, casual permitted
- Can establish business

### Study Rights
- **Unrestricted** - Can study any course
- Domestic fees may apply for some courses
- Some restrictions on student loans

### Medicare
- **Eligible** for Reciprocal Health Care Agreement
- Emergency and essential medical care
- Some restrictions apply
- Private health insurance recommended

### Social Security
- **Protected SCV:** Some benefits available
- **Non-protected SCV:** Limited access
- Family Tax Benefit A may be available to long-term residents

### Voting
- **Cannot vote** in Australian elections (SCV is temporary visa)
- Can vote once Australian citizen

## PATHWAY TO PERMANENT RESIDENCY

While 444 is temporary, pathways to PR exist:

### For Protected SCV Holders
- Treated similarly to PR for many purposes
- Can sponsor family members
- Eligible for citizenship

### For Non-Protected SCV Holders
1. **Citizenship (from 1 July 2023)**
   - 4-year residence requirement
   - Pass citizenship test

2. **Skilled Migration**
   - 189, 190, 491 visas
   - Need skills assessment and points

3. **Employer Sponsored**
   - 482, 186 visas
   - Need employer sponsorship

4. **Family Sponsored**
   - Partner visas (820/801)
   - If in relationship with Australian/PR

5. **Business/Investment**
   - 188, 888 visas
   - For business owners/investors

## DISCLAIMER

The Subclass 444 is granted at the discretion of the border officer. While most NZ citizens are granted automatically, health and character requirements must be met.

Citizenship pathways changed on 1 July 2023. Non-protected SCV holders should verify current requirements before applying.
