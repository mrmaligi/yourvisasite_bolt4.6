# Subclass 602 - Medical Treatment Visa

## Section 1: Executive Overview

**Visa Type:** Temporary Medical Visa  
**Purpose:** Allows individuals to travel to Australia for medical treatment or to support someone receiving treatment  
**Duration:** 3-12 months (depending on circumstances)  
**Cost:** Free to $360 (depending on circumstances)  
**Processing Time:** 14-49 days (varies significantly)

### What is the Subclass 602 Visa?

The Subclass 602 Medical Treatment Visa is a **temporary visa** designed for people who need medical treatment in Australia or want to support someone receiving treatment. This visa can also be used for those donating organs, receiving consultations, or accompanying someone for medical purposes.

### Key Benefits

- **Access to Australian healthcare** - World-class medical facilities
- **Flexible duration** - From 3 months to 12 months
- **Multiple entries** - Travel to and from Australia
- **No work restrictions** - Can work if medically able
- **Study permitted** - Can study for up to 3 months
- **Organ donation** - Special provisions for donors
- **Support person included** - Can bring someone to assist

### Important Limitations

- **Temporary only** - No pathway to permanent residency
- **Treatment purpose only** - Must genuinely seek medical care
- **Health insurance required** - Must maintain adequate coverage
- **No further stay** - Typically cannot extend or change visa onshore

### Who Is This Visa For?

- Individuals seeking medical treatment not available in their country
- People needing organ or tissue donation
- Those accompanying someone for medical treatment
- Individuals receiving medical consultations
- People receiving treatment under agreements between governments

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements

### Core Eligibility Criteria

#### 1. Genuine Medical Need
Must demonstrate a **genuine need** for medical treatment in Australia:
- Treatment is not available or adequate in home country
- Australian medical facilities offer superior care
- Emergency or life-saving treatment required
- Specialized procedures not available elsewhere

#### 2. Medical Documentation
Must provide **comprehensive medical evidence**:
- Diagnosis from home country doctor
- Treatment plan from Australian doctor/medical facility
- Letter from Australian hospital/clinic confirming:
  - Willingness to provide treatment
  - Estimated duration of treatment
  - Cost of treatment
  - Follow-up care requirements

#### 3. Financial Capacity
Must demonstrate **ability to pay** for:
- Medical treatment costs
- Living expenses in Australia
- Travel costs
- Health insurance
- Can use personal funds, sponsorship, or medical insurance

#### 4. Genuine Temporary Entrant
Must demonstrate **intention to leave Australia** after treatment:
- Strong ties to home country
- Evidence of return plans
- No intention to remain permanently
- Compelling reasons to return home

#### 5. Health Insurance
Must maintain **adequate health insurance** for entire stay:
- Overseas Visitor Health Cover (OVHC) recommended
- Or evidence of ability to pay all medical costs
- Insurance must cover planned treatments

### Special Categories

#### Organ Donors
- Streamlined processing for organ/tissue donors
- Donor and recipient can apply together
- Medical evidence required for both parties

#### Support Persons
- Can accompany someone receiving treatment
- Must demonstrate relationship to patient
- Must show genuine need to provide support

#### Medical Evacuation
- For emergency medical situations
- Can be processed urgently
- Often arranged through insurance or medical services

### Additional Requirements

#### Health Requirements
- Must meet Australia's health standards
- Health condition must not pose public health risk
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Must have arrangements for follow-up care

#### Character Requirements
- Must meet Australia's character requirements
- Provide police clearances if requested
- Declare any criminal history

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Obtain Medical Documentation (Weeks 1-4)
- Consult doctor in home country
- Obtain diagnosis and medical reports
- Contact Australian medical facility
- Receive treatment plan and cost estimate
- Get confirmation letter from Australian provider

### Step 2: Arrange Finances (Weeks 2-4)
- Calculate total costs (treatment + living + travel)
- Arrange payment method or medical insurance
- Obtain financial evidence (bank statements, insurance letter)
- If sponsored, obtain sponsorship letter

### Step 3: Arrange Health Insurance (Week 3)
- Purchase Overseas Visitor Health Cover (OVHC)
- Or arrange comprehensive medical insurance
- Ensure coverage includes planned treatments
- Obtain insurance documentation

### Step 4: Create ImmiAccount (Week 4)
- Create or access ImmiAccount
- Prepare for online application

### Step 5: Lodge Application (Week 4)
- Complete online application form
- Pay visa application charge (if applicable)
- Upload all supporting documents
- Submit application

### Step 6: Health Examinations (Weeks 4-6)
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Book through Bupa Medical Visa Services
- Results valid for 12 months

### Step 7: Wait for Processing (Days to Weeks)
- Processing varies: 14 days to 2+ months
- Monitor ImmiAccount for updates
- Respond promptly to any requests

### Step 8: Visa Grant
- Receive grant notification
- Check visa conditions carefully
- Make travel arrangements
- Begin treatment

## Section 7: Key Considerations

### Visa Conditions

The Subclass 602 visa typically includes:
- **Condition 8101:** No work (may be waived if medically able)
- **Condition 8501:** Maintain adequate health insurance
- **Condition 8534:** No further stay (cannot extend or change visa)
- **Condition 8522:** Must not remain in Australia beyond visa validity
- **Condition 8303:** Must not become a charge on public funds

### No Further Stay Condition (8534)

**Important:** Most 602 visas include a "No Further Stay" condition:
- Cannot apply for most other visas while in Australia
- Must leave Australia when visa expires
- Cannot extend stay onshore
- Limited exceptions for compassionate circumstances

### Public Health System

**Important:** 602 visa holders **cannot access**:
- Medicare (Australia's public healthcare)
- Public hospital treatment at subsidized rates
- Must use private healthcare and pay full costs

### Treatment Completion

When treatment is complete:
- Must leave Australia before visa expires
- Can request early visa cessation if treatment finishes early
- Must maintain visa conditions until departure

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Insufficient Medical Evidence
**Problem:** Not providing comprehensive medical documentation.
**Solution:**
- Get detailed diagnosis from home doctor
- Obtain formal letter from Australian hospital
- Include all medical reports and test results
- Explain why treatment is needed in Australia

### Mistake 2: Inadequate Financial Evidence
**Problem:** Not showing ability to pay for treatment.
**Solution:**
- Get detailed cost estimate from hospital
- Show bank statements with sufficient funds
- Arrange medical insurance if possible
- Include evidence of sponsorship if applicable

### Mistake 3: Weak GTE Evidence
**Problem:** Not demonstrating intention to return home.
**Solution:**
- Show strong ties to home country
- Provide return flight booking
- Explain post-treatment plans
- Include employment/family evidence

### Mistake 4: Wrong Health Insurance
**Problem:** Insurance doesn't cover planned treatments.
**Solution:**
- Confirm coverage with insurer before purchasing
- Get written confirmation of coverage
- Ensure insurance covers pre-existing conditions
- Check coverage limits

### Mistake 5: Applying Too Late
**Problem:** Not allowing enough time before treatment needed.
**Solution:**
- Apply at least 2-3 months before treatment
- Processing can take 1-2 months
- Medical emergencies can be expedited
- Plan for potential delays
