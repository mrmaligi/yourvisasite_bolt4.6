# Subclass 407 - Training Visa

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Training Visa (Subclass 407)** is a temporary visa that allows applicants to participate in workplace-based occupational training or professional development activities in Australia. This visa is designed to help individuals improve their skills in their current occupation, area of tertiary study, or field of expertise.

**Key Features:**
- Duration: Up to 2 years
- Work Rights: Full-time work allowed if directly related to approved training
- Study Rights: Permitted
- Pathway to PR: Can lead to other skilled visas (482/186) after gaining experience
- Cost: From AUD $315 - $415

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### Primary Requirements:
- **Age:** Must be at least 18 years of age
- **Sponsorship:** Must be sponsored by an approved Temporary Activities Sponsor
- **Nomination:** Must be nominated by your sponsor for a specific training program
- **English:** Functional English required (IELTS 4.5 or equivalent)
- **Health & Character:** Must meet Australia's health and character requirements
- **Insurance:** Adequate health insurance (OVHC) for entire stay
- **Genuine Intent:** Genuine intention to participate in the training program

### Training Program Types:
1. **Registration Required Training** - For occupations requiring registration/licensing
2. **Occupational Training** - To improve skills in eligible occupations
3. **Professional Development** - For professionals/executives

### Sponsor Requirements:
- Must be a legally operating organisation in Australia
- Must be an Australian government agency, OR
- Foreign government agency with presence in Australia
- Must be approved as a Temporary Activities Sponsor

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Secure Sponsorship
- Identify an approved sponsor willing to nominate you
- Ensure sponsor holds valid Temporary Activities Sponsor approval
- Confirm your training program meets 407 requirements

### Step 2: Sponsor Applies for Nomination
- Sponsor submits nomination application
- Provides detailed training plan
- Demonstrates training benefits your skill development
- Includes evidence of suitable facilities/resources

### Step 3: Gather Documents
- Passport and identity documents
- Evidence of qualifications
- English test results (if required)
- Health insurance confirmation
- Police clearances from countries lived in for 12+ months in last 10 years
- Training plan and nomination approval

### Step 4: Lodge Visa Application
- Create ImmiAccount
- Complete online application form
- Upload all required documents (colour PDFs)
- Pay visa application charge

### Step 5: Health Examinations
- Complete health checks if requested
- Provide biometrics if required

### Step 6: Wait for Decision
- Monitor application status via ImmiAccount
- Respond promptly to any requests for additional information

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes:
1. **Incomplete Training Plan** - Training plan must be detailed and structured
2. **Wrong Sponsor Type** - Sponsor must be approved Temporary Activities Sponsor
3. **Missing Insurance** - Must have valid OVHC for entire stay
4. **Insufficient English Evidence** - Must prove functional English
5. **Delayed Health Checks** - Complete medicals promptly when requested
6. **Outdated Police Clearances** - Ensure clearances are recent
7. **Unclear Training Benefits** - Must clearly show how training improves skills

### Pro Tips:
1. **Choose Reputable Sponsor** - Government agencies and established organisations preferred
2. **Detailed Training Plan** - Include specific outcomes, timelines, and assessment methods
3. **Document Everything** - Keep copies of all training activities and progress
4. **Maintain Insurance** - Ensure continuous OVHC coverage
5. **Check Visa Conditions** - Understand all conditions attached to your visa grant
6. **Pathway Planning** - Use 407 to gain experience for 482/186 pathways
