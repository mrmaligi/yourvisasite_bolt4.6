# Subclass 864 - Contributory Aged Parent (Permanent) Visa

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Contributory Aged Parent (Permanent) Visa (Subclass 864)** allows aged parents of a settled Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen to live in Australia permanently. This visa is for parents who are old enough to receive the age pension in Australia (currently 67 years) and offers permanent residency through the contributory pathway.

**Key Features:**
- **Permanent Visa** - Immediate permanent residency upon grant
- **Age Requirement** - Must be old enough for Australian age pension
- **Onshore Application** - Must be in Australia to apply (but no bridging visa granted)
- **Work Rights** - Full work rights from grant
- **Medicare** - Eligible for Medicare
- **Cost:** High (similar to other contributory parent visas)
- **Processing:** Subject to capping and queueing

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### Primary Requirements:
- **Age:** Must be old enough to receive the age pension in Australia (currently 67 years for those born after 1957)
- **Parent Relationship:** Must be parent of Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- **Child Status:** Child must be "settled" in Australia (generally 2+ years residence)
- **Balance of Family Test:** At least half of children must live in Australia, or more children in Australia than any other country
- **Location:** Must be in Australia (but NOT in immigration clearance) when applying and when visa is granted
- **Health & Character:** Must meet requirements
- **Assurance of Support:** Required (financial bond)
- **No Outstanding Debts:** No debts to Australian Government

### Age Requirements (Age Pension Age):
| Date of Birth | Age Required |
|---------------|-------------|
| Before 1 July 1952 | 65 years |
| 1 July 1952 - 31 Dec 1953 | 65 years 6 months |
| 1 Jan 1954 - 30 Jun 1955 | 66 years |
| 1 Jul 1955 - 31 Dec 1956 | 66 years 6 months |
| From 1 January 1957 | 67 years |

### Balance of Family Test:
- At least half of children must be eligible children (living in Australia as citizens/PRs), OR
- More eligible children living in Australia than in any other single country
- Includes step-children
- Children who have passed away may be excluded from calculation

### Sponsor Requirements:
- Must be child (or spouse/de facto partner of child)
- Must be Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- Must be settled in Australia (usually 2+ years)
- Must be 18+ years
- Can be joint sponsor with another eligible child

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Confirm Eligibility
- Verify you meet age requirement
- Check balance of family test
- Confirm sponsor's settled status
- Ensure you are in Australia

### Step 2: Understand the Queue System
- Parent visas subject to capping and queueing
- Applications assigned queue date
- Wait for queue date to be reached
- Check current queue release dates

### Step 3: Prepare Documents
- Identity and age documents
- Relationship evidence
- Balance of family evidence
- Sponsor documents
- Health and character documents

### Step 4: Submit Application
- Complete Form 47PA
- Must be in Australia when lodging
- Pay first installment
- NO bridging visa granted (must maintain lawful status)

### Step 5: Wait in Queue
- Application placed in queue
- Must maintain lawful status in Australia
- May need to apply for other visas (e.g., tourist visa) while waiting

### Step 6: Second Installment Request
- When application reaches front of queue
- Pay second installment
- Provide assurance of support

### Step 7: Final Processing
- Health and character checks
- Final documentation
- Visa grant (permanent residency)

### Step 8: After Grant
- Permanent resident immediately
- Medicare eligibility
- Work and study rights
- Pathway to citizenship

## 6. Processing Times (Official)

### Queue System:
Parent visas operate on a capping and queueing system:
- **Capping:** Maximum number granted each program year
- **Queueing:** Applications processed in order of queue date

### Current Queue Status (2025):
- Applications from 2017-2018 currently being processed
- New applications joining the queue
- Processing time varies based on queue position

### Estimated Timeline:
| Stage | Estimated Time |
|-------|---------------|
| **Queue Assignment** | Immediate upon lodgement |
| **Queue Waiting** | 5-7 years |
| **Final Processing** | 6-12 months |
| **Total** | 5-8 years |

### Processing Priorities:
- Contributory visas processed faster than non-contributory
- All aged and standard contributory parent visas share the same queue
- Queue release dates published quarterly

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes:
1. **Age Requirement Not Met** - Must meet pension age before applying
2. **Not in Australia** - Must be onshore to apply
3. **Balance of Family Test Failure** - Must meet strict requirements
4. **No Lawful Status** - Must maintain lawful status while waiting (no bridging visa)
5. **Insufficient Evidence** - Clear proof of relationship and age needed
6. **Assurance of Support Issues** - Bond must be provided correctly

### Pro Tips:
1. **Check Age Requirement First** - Ensure you meet pension age
2. **Maintain Lawful Status** - Critical - no bridging visa granted
3. **Consider 884 First** - May spread costs by applying for temporary visa first
4. **Balance of Family Check** - Verify test met before applying
5. **Sponsor Settlement** - Ensure sponsor meets 2-year requirement
6. **Financial Preparation** - Save for high fees
7. **Assurance of Support** - Arrange bond early
8. **Queue Monitoring** - Check queue release dates regularly

**No Bridging Visa:**
- 864 applicants do NOT receive bridging visas
- Must maintain lawful status while waiting
- May need to apply for tourist visas repeatedly
- Plan for 5-7 years of status maintenance

**Age Requirement:**
- Must meet age pension age at application
- Cannot apply before reaching required age
- Check current age requirements carefully
