# Subclass 403 - Temporary Work (International Relations) Visa

## Section 1: Executive Overview

**Visa Type:** Temporary Work Visa (Multiple Streams)  
**Purpose:** Allows work in Australia under international agreements or government programs  
**Duration:** Up to 2 years (varies by stream)  
**Cost:** AUD $310 (base application charge)  
**Processing Time:** 21-60 days (varies by stream)

### What is the Subclass 403 Visa?

The Subclass 403 Temporary Work (International Relations) Visa is a **flexible temporary work visa** with multiple streams designed for specific international arrangements. It covers diplomatic staff, government agreement workers, seasonal workers, and those participating in special programs that enhance Australia's international relations.

### Available Streams

1. **Foreign Government Agency Stream** - Diplomatic and consular staff
2. **Domestic Worker (Diplomatic/Consular) Stream** - Domestic staff of diplomatic missions
3. **Seasonal Worker Program Stream** - Pacific island workers in agriculture
4. **Pacific Australia Labour Mobility (PALM) Stream** - Pacific workers in various sectors
5. **International Representative Stream** - International organization staff

### Key Benefits

- **Multiple streams** - Various pathways for different situations
- **Work rights** - Can work in specified occupation
- **Study permitted** - Can study for up to 3 months
- **Multiple entries** - Travel to and from Australia
- **Family included** - Can include family members (some streams)
- **No skills assessment** - For most streams
- **Simplified requirements** - For eligible streams

### Important Limitations

- **Stream-specific conditions** - Different rules apply to each stream
- **Limited work rights** - Must work only in approved occupation
- **Temporary only** - No pathway to permanent residency
- **Sponsorship required** - Most streams require Australian sponsor

### Who Is This Visa For?

- Diplomatic and consular staff
- Domestic workers for diplomatic missions
- Seasonal agricultural workers from Pacific islands
- PALM scheme participants
- International organization representatives
- Staff under government-to-government agreements

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements by Stream

### Stream 1: Foreign Government Agency

**For:** Diplomatic staff, consular officers, official representatives

#### Requirements:
- Employed by foreign government agency
- Working in Australia in official capacity
- Hold position with appropriate privileges and immunities
- Support from Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT)

**Duration:** Duration of posting (typically 1-4 years)

### Stream 2: Domestic Worker (Diplomatic/Consular)

**For:** Domestic workers employed by diplomatic or consular staff

#### Requirements:
- Employed by diplomatic or consular officer
- Work as domestic helper, cook, cleaner, or caregiver
- Employed by person with appropriate visa/status
- Support from DFAT

**Duration:** Up to 2 years

### Stream 3: Seasonal Worker Program

**For:** Workers from Pacific islands and Timor-Leste for seasonal agricultural work

#### Requirements:
- Citizen of participating Pacific country or Timor-Leste
- Selected by approved employer
- Meet health and character requirements
- Return home after seasonal work

**Participating Countries:** Fiji, Kiribati, Nauru, Papua New Guinea, Samoa, Solomon Islands, Timor-Leste, Tonga, Tuvalu, Vanuatu

**Duration:** Up to 9 months

### Stream 4: Pacific Australia Labour Mobility (PALM)

**For:** Workers from Pacific countries and Timor-Leste for various industries

#### Requirements:
- Citizen of participating Pacific country or Timor-Leste
- Selected by approved employer
- Offer of employment in approved sector
- Meet health and character requirements

**Duration:** Up to 4 years (with possible extension)

### Stream 5: International Representative

**For:** Staff of international organizations or those with international status

#### Requirements:
- Hold position with international organization
- Have appropriate privileges and immunities
- Letter of support from organization
- Meet health and character requirements

**Duration:** Duration of appointment

### Common Requirements (All Streams)

#### Health Requirements
- Meet Australia's health standards
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Must not pose public health risk

#### Character Requirements
- Meet Australia's character requirements
- Provide police clearances if requested
- Declare any criminal history

#### Financial Capacity
- Must demonstrate ability to support yourself
- Or evidence of sponsor support
- Adequate health insurance

#### Genuine Temporary Entrant
- Must demonstrate intention to stay temporarily
- Strong ties to home country (for worker streams)
- Willingness to comply with visa conditions

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Determine Correct Stream (Week 1)
- Identify which stream applies to your situation
- Check specific requirements for that stream
- Confirm eligibility
- Gather stream-specific documentation

### Step 2: Obtain Sponsorship/Approval (Weeks 1-4)

**For Government/Diplomatic Streams:**
- Obtain DFAT support letter
- Get approval from employing agency

**For PALM/Seasonal Worker Streams:**
- Apply through approved employer
- Complete employer selection process
- Obtain job offer from approved sponsor

**For International Representative:**
- Get letter from international organization
- Confirm privileges and immunities status

### Step 3: Gather Documents (Weeks 2-4)
- Stream-specific documentation
- Identity documents
- Health and character documents
- Financial evidence

### Step 4: Create ImmiAccount (Week 4)
- Create or access ImmiAccount
- Prepare for online application

### Step 5: Lodge Application (Week 4)
- Complete online application form
- Pay visa application charge (AUD $310)
- Upload all supporting documents
- Submit application

### Step 6: Health Examinations (Weeks 4-6)
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Book through Bupa Medical Visa Services

### Step 7: Wait for Processing (Weeks 4-8)
- Processing varies by stream:
  - PALM/Seasonal Worker: 2-4 weeks
  - Diplomatic: 3-6 weeks
  - Other: 4-8 weeks
- Monitor ImmiAccount for updates

### Step 8: Visa Grant
- Receive grant notification
- Check visa conditions carefully
- Make travel arrangements

## Section 7: Key Considerations

### Visa Conditions by Stream

#### Seasonal Worker Program:
- Work only for approved employer
- Work only in approved location
- Must leave Australia when work complete
- Cannot bring family members
- Must maintain adequate health insurance

#### PALM Stream:
- Work only for approved employer
- Can work in various approved sectors
- Can bring family (certain circumstances)
- Can extend visa (eligibility permitting)
- Must maintain health insurance

#### Diplomatic/Consular Streams:
- Work only for sponsoring agency
- Privileges and immunities apply
- Family can be included
- Diplomatic protocols apply
- Duration linked to posting

### No Further Stay Condition

Some streams include "No Further Stay" condition:
- Cannot apply for most other visas while in Australia
- Must leave Australia when visa expires
- Limited exceptions for compelling circumstances

### Work Rights

- Must work only in approved occupation
- Must work only for approved employer (most streams)
- Cannot work for other employers
- Must notify DHA of any changes

### Family Members

Rules vary by stream:
- **PALM:** Family allowed in some circumstances
- **Diplomatic:** Family generally allowed
- **Seasonal Worker:** Family not permitted
- Check specific stream requirements

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Wrong Stream Selection
**Problem:** Applying under incorrect stream.
**Solution:**
- Carefully review all stream requirements
- Contact DHA if unsure
- Ensure correct documentation for chosen stream
- Check employer/sponsor eligibility

### Mistake 2: Missing Sponsor Approval
**Problem:** Employer/sponsor not approved or not providing required support.
**Solution:**
- Confirm sponsor is approved for the stream
- Obtain all required letters and documentation
- Ensure DFAT support (diplomatic streams)
- Complete PALM/Seasonal Worker selection process

### Mistake 3: Insufficient Citizenship Evidence
**Problem:** Not proving eligibility for PALM/Seasonal Worker streams.
**Solution:**
- Provide passport from participating country
- Check country eligibility list
- Ensure passport validity
- Provide birth certificate if needed

### Mistake 4: Wrong Documentation
**Problem:** Providing generic work documents instead of stream-specific evidence.
**Solution:**
- Use stream-specific document checklist
- Obtain correct letters and approvals
- Ensure documents match stream requirements
- Check DHA website for current requirements

### Mistake 5: Not Understanding Conditions
**Problem:** Not aware of specific visa conditions.
**Solution:**
- Read visa conditions carefully
- Understand work limitations
- Know when visa expires
- Understand restrictions on family
