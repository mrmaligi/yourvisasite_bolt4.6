# Subclass 489 - Skilled Regional (Provisional) Visa

> **VISA STATUS:** LEGACY VISA - Closed to new applications (replaced by Subclass 491)
> 
> **Last Updated:** March 2025

---

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Skilled Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 489)** was a temporary visa designed for skilled workers who wanted to live and work in specified regional areas of Australia. This visa has been **closed to new applications** and was replaced by the **Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491)** in November 2019.

### Key Features (Historical)
- **Duration:** 4 years from the date of grant
- **Pathway to Permanent Residency:** Eligible to apply for Subclass 887 (Skilled Regional) after meeting residence requirements
- **Regional Requirement:** Must live and work in specified regional areas
- **Family Inclusion:** Could include family members in the application

### Three Pathways (Historical)
1. **Invited Pathway:** For skilled workers nominated by a state/territory or sponsored by eligible relatives
2. **Extended Pathway:** For holders of certain provisional visas seeking extension
3. **Subsequent Entry:** For family members of existing 489 visa holders

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements (Historical)

### For Invited Pathway:
- **Age:** Under 45 years at time of invitation
- **Occupation:** Occupation on the relevant skilled occupation list
- **Skills Assessment:** Positive skills assessment for the nominated occupation
- **Points Test:** Score at least 65 points on the points test
- **English:** Competent English (IELTS 6.0 or equivalent)
- **Health & Character:** Meet health and character requirements
- **Sponsorship:** Either:
  - Nominated by an Australian state/territory government, OR
  - Sponsored by an eligible relative living in a designated area

### Regional Areas:
- All of Australia except Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Gold Coast, and Perth
- Specific postcode requirements applied

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Process (Historical Reference)

### Step 1: Skills Assessment
- Obtain positive skills assessment from relevant assessing authority
- Ensure occupation is on the applicable skilled occupation list

### Step 2: Submit Expression of Interest (EOI)
- Create SkillSelect account
- Submit EOI indicating interest in 489 visa
- Specify state/territory nomination or family sponsorship preference

### Step 3: Receive Invitation
- Wait for invitation from Department of Home Affairs
- Invitations issued based on points score and occupation demand

### Step 4: Lodge Application
- Submit visa application within 60 days of invitation
- Pay visa application charge
- Upload all supporting documents

### Step 5: Health and Character Checks
- Complete medical examinations
- Provide police clearance certificates

### Step 6: Visa Decision
- Wait for processing and decision
- If granted, visa valid for 4 years

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Historical Mistakes to Avoid:
1. **Incorrect ANZSCO code** - Ensure occupation code matches skills assessment
2. **Expired documents** - Police checks valid for 12 months only
3. **Incomplete EOI** - All claims must be supported by evidence
4. **Missing relationship evidence** - De facto relationships needed 12 months evidence
5. **Incorrect sponsor location** - Family sponsor must live in designated area

### Tips for Current Applicants (Subclass 491):
- The Subclass 491 replaced the 489 with similar but updated requirements
- Regional areas definition has expanded
- Points test minimum increased to 65
- Three-year pathway to PR (subclass 191)

### 10C. Timeline Factors (Historical Analysis)

**Fastest Processing:**
- State/territory nominated applications
- Medical/nursing occupations (critical shortage)
- Complete documentation at lodgement
- Simple cases with no dependents

**Slowest Processing:**
- Family sponsored applications (additional verification)
- Complex family compositions
- Character concerns requiring additional checks
- Health conditions requiring waivers

**Common Delay Reasons:**
1. Incomplete documentation (30% of delays)
2. Health assessments requiring follow-up (20%)
3. Character checks from multiple countries (15%)
4. Verification of employment claims (15%)
5. Peak processing periods (10%)

### 10D. Current Processing Trends (2024-2025)

**IMPORTANT:** The Subclass 489 is **closed to new applications**.

Current holders and those transitioning:
- Existing 489 visas remain valid until expiry
- Subclass 887 applications (pathway to PR) still being processed
- Processing times for 887: 12-24 months currently

**Transition to Subclass 491:**
- Similar processing patterns observed
- State nominations generally faster than family sponsored
- 491 processing currently: 3-12 months depending on stream
