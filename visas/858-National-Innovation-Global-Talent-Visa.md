# Subclass 858 - National Innovation Visa (formerly Global Talent)

## Section 1: Executive Overview

**Visa Type:** Permanent Resident Visa  
**Purpose:** For internationally recognized individuals with exceptional talent in priority sectors  
**Duration:** Permanent - indefinite stay  
**Cost:** AUD $4,765 (base application charge)  
**Processing Time:** 3-6 months (fast-track processing)

### What is the Subclass 858 Visa?

The Subclass 858 National Innovation Visa (formerly Global Talent Visa) is a **premium permanent residency pathway** for highly skilled individuals who have achieved **international recognition** in their field. This visa replaced the Global Talent Independent (GTI) program and offers **fast-track processing** for exceptional talent in Australia's priority sectors.

### Key Benefits

- **Permanent residency immediately** - No provisional stage
- **Fast processing** - 3-6 months (vs 12+ months for other visas)
- **No points test** - No age, English, or points requirements
- **No skills assessment** - Recognition based on achievements
- **Work anywhere** - No regional restrictions
- **Family included** - Partner and children granted permanent residence
- **Medicare access** - Immediate access to public healthcare
- **Citizenship pathway** - Eligible for citizenship after 4 years

### Who Is This Visa For?

- Internationally recognized professionals and researchers
- Leaders in priority sectors (see list below)
- High-income earners ($160,000+ AUD or ability to earn)
- Individuals with exceptional and outstanding achievements
- Those who can demonstrate benefit to Australia

### Priority Sectors (2025)

1. **Resources** (mining, energy, resources technology)
2. **Agri-food and AgTech** (agricultural technology)
3. **Energy** (renewables, clean energy, hydrogen)
4. **Health Industries** (biotech, medical research, pharmaceuticals)
5. **Defence, Advanced Manufacturing and Space** (defence tech, aerospace)
6. **Circular Economy** (waste management, recycling, sustainability)
7. **DigiTech** (AI, cybersecurity, data science, quantum computing)
8. **Infrastructure and Tourism** (major projects, sustainable tourism)
9. **Financial Services and FinTech** (fintech, blockchain, regtech)
10. **Education** (research, educational technology)

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements

### Core Eligibility Criteria

#### 1. Internationally Recognized Record
Must demonstrate **exceptional and outstanding achievement** in one of the priority sectors:
- International awards and recognition
- Significant contributions to field
- Leadership in major projects or organizations
- Patents, publications, or innovations
- High-level professional standing

#### 2. Current Income OR Future Earning Capacity
Must demonstrate one of the following:
- **Current income** of at least AUD $160,000 per year (or foreign equivalent)
- **Ability to earn** at least AUD $160,000 in Australia
- Recent PhD graduates in priority sectors may be exempt

#### 3. Nominator or Sponsor
Must be **nominated** by one of the following:
- Australian citizen or permanent resident in same field
- New Zealand citizen in same field
- Australian organization with national reputation in the sector

#### 4. Benefit to Australia
Must demonstrate how your presence will benefit Australia:
- Economic contribution
- Innovation and research advancement
- Job creation
- Sector development

### Additional Requirements

#### Health Requirements
- Meet Australia's health standards
- Complete health examinations if requested
- Family members must also meet health requirements

#### Character Requirements
- Meet Australia's character requirements
- Provide police clearances if requested

#### No Age Limit
- No age restriction (unlike most skilled visas)
- Older applicants with exceptional achievements welcome

#### No English Requirement
- No formal English language test required
- But functional English is expected for workplace

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Application Process

### Step 1: Assess Eligibility (Weeks 1-2)
- Determine if you meet the high achievement standard
- Confirm your field is in priority sectors
- Gather evidence of international recognition
- Identify potential nominator

### Step 2: Find a Nominator (Weeks 2-4)
- Approach Australian professional in your field
- Or contact Australian organization in your sector
- Nominator must complete Form 1000
- Strong nomination letter is critical

### Step 3: Submit Expression of Interest (EOI) (Week 4)
- Create ImmiAccount
- Submit EOI through Global Talent portal
- Include Form 1000 (nomination)
- Upload evidence of achievements

### Step 4: Wait for Invitation (Weeks 4-12)
- DHA reviews EOI
- If successful, receive invitation to apply
- EOI remains active for 2 years if not invited

### Step 5: Lodge Visa Application (Within 60 days of invitation)
- Complete visa application
- Pay application fee (AUD $4,765)
- Upload all supporting documents
- Submit within invitation validity period

### Step 6: Health and Character (Weeks 1-4 after application)
- Complete health examinations
- Provide police clearances
- Biometrics if requested

### Step 7: Visa Grant (Months 2-6)
- Fast-track processing typically 3-6 months
- Receive grant notification
- Begin planning your move to Australia

## Section 7: Key Considerations

### Understanding "Exceptional and Outstanding"

This is the **highest bar** for any Australian visa. Examples include:
- Nobel Prize winners
- Olympic medalists
- CEOs of major international companies
- Leading researchers with h-index >30
- Inventors of breakthrough technologies
- World-renowned artists and musicians

However, it's not limited to household names. You may qualify if you:
- Are in top 5-10% of your field globally
- Have significant industry recognition
- Hold senior positions in multinational companies
- Have received prestigious awards
- Are cited extensively in your field

### The Nominator's Role

The nominator is **critical** to your application:
- Must be recognized in same field
- Must attest to your exceptional status
- Cannot be a paid migration agent
- Should have credible standing

Finding a nominator:
- Professional networks and conferences
- LinkedIn outreach to Australian professionals
- Australian university contacts
- Industry associations

### Income Threshold Nuances

- **$160,000** is the target, not minimum
- Can include bonuses and equity
- Future earning potential considered
- Regional salary adjustments not applicable
- Recent PhDs in priority sectors may be exempt

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Mistake 1: Insufficient Evidence of Achievement
**Problem:** Application shows good but not exceptional achievement.
**Solution:**
- Focus on quantifiable impact
- Include international comparisons
- Get strong testimonials from recognized figures
- Demonstrate leadership in field

### Mistake 2: Weak Nominator
**Problem:** Nominator not recognized or credible in the field.
**Solution:**
- Choose nominator with strong credentials
- Ensure they understand the "exceptional" standard
- Provide them with your full evidence package
- Consider multiple potential nominators

### Mistake 3: Wrong Sector Alignment
**Problem:** Work doesn't clearly fit priority sectors.
**Solution:**
- Carefully map your work to priority sectors
- Explain sector relevance clearly
- Focus on innovation aspects

### Mistake 4: Incomplete EOI
**Problem:** Rushing EOI without strong evidence.
**Solution:**
- Take time to gather all evidence
- Write compelling personal statement
- Ensure all documents support claims

### Mistake 5: Ignoring the "Benefit to Australia"
**Problem:** Not explaining how you'll contribute to Australia.
**Solution:**
- Research Australian industry needs
- Propose specific contributions
- Show understanding of Australian context
- Include letters of support from Australian organizations

### 10F. How to Track Your Application

**EOI Status:**
- Check through ImmiAccount
- No updates until decision
- Can remain "Received" for months

**Visa Application Status:**
- **Received:** Application lodged successfully
- **Initial Assessment:** Documents being reviewed
- **Further Assessment:** Additional checks occurring
- **Finalised:** Decision made

**When to Follow Up:**
- EOI: After 3 months if no invitation
- Visa: After 6 months if no decision

### 10G. Tips to Speed Up Processing

1. **Strong EOI package** - Comprehensive evidence upfront
2. **Credible nominator** - Well-known in Australian sector
3. **Clear sector alignment** - Explicitly link to priority sector
4. **Quantified achievements** - Numbers, impact, outcomes
5. **Australian connections** - Letters of support, job offers
6. **Complete application** - All documents with initial lodgement

### 10H. If Your Application is Delayed

**EOI Not Invited:**
- EOI remains active for 2 years
- Can update with new achievements
- Consider alternative visa pathways
- Seek feedback if possible

**Visa Application Delayed:**
- Submit enquiry through ImmiAccount
- Check if additional documents needed
- Ensure health/character checks completed
