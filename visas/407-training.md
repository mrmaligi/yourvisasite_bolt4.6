# Subclass 407 - Training Visa

> **VISA STATUS:** CURRENTLY OPEN
> > **Last Updated:** March 2025

---

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Training visa (subclass 407)** allows individuals to participate in workplace-based occupational training activities in Australia to improve their skills for their job, area of tertiary study, field of expertise, or in a professional development training program.

### Key Features
- **Duration:** Up to 2 years
- **Work Rights:** Limited to training activities specified in visa grant
- **Study Rights:** Permitted in line with training objectives
- **Family:** Family members can be included
- **Pathway:** Can lead to other skilled or employer-sponsored visas

### Types of Occupational Training
1. **Type 1:** Registration-required training
2. **Type 2:** Structured workplace-based training
3. **Type 3:** Professional development training

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### For Visa Applicants

#### Basic Requirements
- **Age:** Generally 18+ (exceptions apply for specific training types)
- **Sponsorship:** Must have an approved sponsor
- **Nomination:** Must be nominated by the sponsor
- **Health Insurance:** Must maintain adequate health insurance
- **Health & Character:** Meet standard requirements
- **Genuine Temporary Entrant:** Must genuinely intend to stay temporarily

#### Skills/Qualifications Requirements
- Must have appropriate qualifications and/or experience for the training
- Letter from regulatory body confirming qualifications may be required

#### English Language Requirements
- **Functional English** required (IELTS 4.5 overall or equivalent)
- Some streams may require higher English proficiency

### For Sponsors

#### Eligible Sponsors
- Australian businesses operating lawfully
- Australian government agencies
- Foreign government agencies with Australian presence
- Commonwealth entities (some exemptions apply)

#### Sponsorship Obligations
- Provide occupational training
- Ensure training meets regulatory requirements
- Comply with sponsorship conditions
- Notify DHA of certain changes

### For Nomination

#### Training Requirements
- Training must be structured and supervised
- Must be time-limited
- Must be genuinely for training purposes (not regular employment)
- Must improve skills in eligible occupation

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Find a Sponsor
- Identify Australian organisation willing to provide training
- Sponsor must be approved or apply for sponsorship approval

### Step 2: Sponsorship Application (if required)
- Sponsor applies for approval (Standard Business Sponsorship or equivalent)
- Processing time: 1-4 weeks for Commonwealth entities

### Step 3: Nomination
- Sponsor nominates applicant for specific training program
- Submit detailed training plan
- Pay nomination fee

### Step 4: Gather Documents
- Prepare personal and professional documents
- Obtain health insurance
- Complete health examinations
- Obtain police clearances

### Step 5: Visa Application
- Create or log into ImmiAccount
- Complete online application
- Pay visa application charge
- Upload all supporting documents

### Step 6: Decision
- Wait for processing
- Respond to any requests for additional information
- Receive visa grant notification

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes
1. **Insufficient training plan** - Plan must be detailed and structured
2. **Regular employment disguised as training** - Will result in refusal
3. **Wrong sponsor type** - Not all organisations can sponsor
4. **Inadequate health insurance** - Must maintain OVHC for entire stay
5. **Missing regulatory body letter** - Required for some occupations
6. **Incorrect visa condition understanding** - 8102 condition restricts work to training only

### Tips for Success
1. **Develop comprehensive training plan** - Include objectives, supervision details, duration
2. **Ensure genuine training need** - Document why training is necessary
3. **Choose appropriate sponsor** - Commonwealth entities have streamlined processes
4. **Maintain health insurance** - Arrange before arrival
5. **Understand visa conditions** - 8102 restricts work to specified training
6. **Prepare for 40-hour work limit** - Secondary applicants limited to 40 hours per fortnight

### Important Conditions
- **Condition 8102:** Can only work in relation to training
- **Condition 8501:** Must maintain health insurance
- **Condition 8534:** No further stay (may apply in some cases)

### 10C. Timeline Factors

**Fastest Processing:**
- Commonwealth entity sponsors (universities, government agencies)
- Well-structured training plans
- Health and character clear from low-risk countries
- Complete documentation at lodgement
- Professional/regulatory body endorsement

**Slowest Processing:**
- Private business sponsors (additional verification)
- Complex training arrangements
- Countries requiring additional security checks
- Incomplete training plans
- High-risk occupations

**Common Delay Reasons:**
1. Incomplete training plan details (30%)
2. Sponsor verification delays (20%)
3. Health examination follow-ups (15%)
4. Character checks from certain countries (15%)
5. Additional information requests (20%)

2. **Submit detailed training plan** - Include objectives, supervision, assessment
3. **Complete health checks early** - Use myHealthDeclaraed

6. **Maintain clear communication** - Respond promptly to any requests

### 10H. If Your Application is Delayed

**Actions to Take:**
1. Check ImmiAccount for any requests for information
2. Ensure sponsor has maintained eligibility
3. Verify health insurance remains current
4. Contact Global Service Centre if exceeding 90th percentile
5. Consider engaging migration agent for complex cases

**When to Escalate:**
- Exceeding published 90% processing time
- Urgent compassionate circumstances
- Sponsor circumstances changing
