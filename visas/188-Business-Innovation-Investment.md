# Subclass 188 - Business Innovation and Investment (Provisional) Visa

## Section 1: Executive Overview

**IMPORTANT UPDATE (2024):** The Business Innovation and Investment Program (BIIP) closed to new applicants on July 31, 2024. This visa guide is maintained for existing applicants and those seeking to understand the program structure.

The Business Innovation and Investment (Provisional) visa (subclass 188) was designed for business owners, investors, and entrepreneurs who wanted to establish or manage a business in Australia.

**Key Features (Historical):**
- **Visa Type:** Provisional (4 years)
- **Pathway to PR:** Led to Subclass 888
- **Streams:** Business Innovation, Investor, Significant Investor, Premium Investor
- **Points Tested:** Minimum 65 points required

**Current Status:**
- Applications lodged before July 31, 2024 continue to be processed
- No new applications accepted
- Replacement program under review

---

## Section 2: Eligibility Requirements (Historical)

### Stream Requirements (Historical)

**Business Innovation Stream:**
- Own or manage business with turnover AUD $750,000+
- Net personal/business assets AUD $1.25M+
- Score 65+ points
- Under 55 years (exceptions possible)

**Investor Stream:**
- Designated investment of AUD $2.5M+
- Net assets AUD $2.5M+
- Score 65+ points
- Under 55 years (exceptions possible)

**Significant Investor Stream:**
- Investment of AUD $5M in complying investments
- No age limit
- No English requirement
- No points test

**Premium Investor Stream:**
- Investment of AUD $15M+
- Invitation only
- Austrade nomination required

### Common Requirements

1. **EOI and Nomination**
   - Submit Expression of Interest via SkillSelect
   - Receive nomination from state/territory government or Austrade

2. **Points Test**
   - Minimum 65 points (most streams)
   - Points for age, business experience, assets, etc.

3. **Health and Character**
   - Meet health requirements
   - Meet character requirements

- Business ownership documents

### Investor Stream

## Section 6: Current Status

### Program Closure

**Closure Date:** July 31, 2024

**Reasons for Closure:**
- Program review by Productivity Commission
- Low economic benefit per dollar invested
- Focus shifting to skilled migration

**Existing Applicants:**
- Applications continue to be processed
- No refunds for lodged applications
- Can still transition to Subclass 888

### Future Program

The Australian Government is reviewing options for a new business visa program with focus on:
- Innovation and entrepreneurship
- Job creation
- Economic benefit to Australia

No timeline announced for replacement program.

---

## Section 7: Transition to Subclass 888

### Eligibility for Permanent Visa

188 visa holders can apply for Subclass 888 after:
- **Business Innovation:** 3 years (was 2 years prior to 2024 changes)
- **Investor:** 4 years
- **Significant Investor:** 3 years (or 4 years depending on investment timing)

### Requirements for 888

- Meet residence requirements
- Maintain business/investment obligations
- Meet business performance criteria
- Health and character requirements

---

## Section 8: Alternatives to 188

### For Business Owners

1. **Subclass 482 (Skills in Demand)**
   - Employer sponsorship required
   - Business can sponsor themselves

2. **Subclass 186 (Employer Nomination)**
   - Direct entry pathway
   - Requires business operation

3. **Subclass 190/491 (Skilled Nominated)**
   - If eligible for skilled occupation
   - Business owner may qualify

### For Investors

1. **Subclass 890/891/892/893 (Closed)**
   - Previous investor visa programs (all closed)

2. **Consider Other Countries**
   - Canada Start-up Visa
   - UK Innovator Visa
   - Singapore Global Investor Program
