# Subclass 820/801 - Partner Visa (Onshore)

## 1. Executive Overview

The **Partner Visa (Subclasses 820 and 801)** allows the spouse or de facto partner of an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible New Zealand citizen to live in Australia. This is a combined application where applicants first receive the temporary 820 visa, followed by the permanent 801 visa approximately 2 years later.

**Key Features:**
- **Subclass 820:** Temporary partner visa (granted first)
- **Subclass 801:** Permanent partner visa (granted approximately 2 years after 820)
- **Lodgement:** Must be in Australia (onshore)
- **Work Rights:** Full work rights from 820 grant
- **Medicare:** Eligible for Medicare on 820
- **Cost:** ~AUD $9,095 - $9,365 (combined application)

---

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### Primary Requirements:
- **Relationship:** Must be spouse or de facto partner of Australian citizen, permanent resident, or eligible NZ citizen
- **Age:** Both partners must be 18+ (limited exceptions apply)
- **Location:** Must be in Australia when applying and when 820 is granted
- **Relationship Evidence:** Must prove genuine and continuing relationship
- **Health & Character:** Must meet requirements
- **Sponsor:** Partner must be approved as sponsor
- **Previous Visa Compliance:** Must not have visa cancellation history (waivers available)

### Relationship Requirements:

**Married Partners:**
- Valid marriage certificate
- Marriage must be valid under Australian law
- Must demonstrate genuine relationship

**De Facto Partners:**
- Must have lived together for 12 months (unless registered relationship)
- Must demonstrate commitment, shared life, exclusivity
- Can be same-sex or opposite-sex

### Four Aspects of Relationship (Must demonstrate ALL):
1. **Financial Aspects** - Joint finances, shared expenses
2. **Nature of Household** - Living arrangements, shared responsibilities
3. **Social Context** - Recognition as couple by friends/family
4. **Commitment** - Long-term intention, exclusivity

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Confirm Eligibility
- Verify relationship meets criteria
- Check sponsor eligibility
- Gather preliminary evidence

### Step 2: Prepare Relationship Evidence
**Financial:**
- Joint bank accounts
- Joint loans/mortgages
- Joint bills/utilities
- Evidence of financial support

**Household:**
- Joint lease/mortgage
- Shared household bills
- Evidence of shared responsibilities
- Mail addressed to both partners

**Social:**
- Photos together over time
- Travel documents together
- Statements from friends/family
- Social media evidence

**Commitment:**
- Timeline of relationship
- Future plans together
- Evidence of exclusivity

### Step 3: Sponsor Application
- Sponsor completes sponsorship form
- Police checks for sponsor
- Evidence of Australian status

### Step 4: Gather Documents
- Identity documents
- Relationship evidence
- Health examinations
- Police clearances
- Character documents

### Step 5: Lodge Application
- Create ImmiAccount
- Complete online application
- Upload all documents
- Pay visa application charge

### Step 6: After Lodgement
- Complete health examinations
- Provide biometrics if requested
- Respond to requests for information
- Wait for 820 processing

### Step 7: 820 Grant (Temporary)
- Receive temporary partner visa
- Full work and study rights
- Medicare eligibility
- Can travel in and out of Australia

### Step 8: 801 Application (Approximately 2 Years Later)
- Immigration contacts for 801 processing
- Provide updated relationship evidence
- Demonstrate ongoing commitment
- 801 grant (permanent residency)

### Step 9: Citizenship (Optional)
- After 4 years on 820/801 (including 1 year as permanent resident)
- Apply for Australian citizenship

## 8. Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes:
1. **Insufficient Relationship Evidence** - Need comprehensive proof across all four aspects
2. **Poor Quality Photos** - Photos should be dated and show progression
3. **Missing AFP Check** - Mandatory for sponsor
4. **Inconsistent Statements** - Partners' statements should align
5. **Late Health Checks** - Complete medicals promptly
6. **Expired Police Clearances** - Must be recent
7. **Incomplete Forms** - Form 80 must be thorough
8. **Not Updating Address** - Must notify of changes within required timeframe

### Pro Tips:
1. **Start Gathering Evidence Early** - Collect evidence throughout relationship
2. **Organise by Categories** - Financial, household, social, commitment
3. **Date Everything** - Show progression over time
4. **Statutory Declarations** - Get statements from Australian citizens/PRs
5. **Sponsor Preparation** - Ensure sponsor has clean record
6. **Complete Medicals Early** - Don't wait to be asked
7. **Respond Promptly** - Reply to requests within specified timeframe
8. **Keep Copies** - Maintain copies of everything submitted
9. **Stay Informed** - Check processing times and policy changes
10. **Consider Agent** - Complex cases may benefit from professional help
