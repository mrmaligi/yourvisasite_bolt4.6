# Subclass 417 - Working Holiday Visa

## Section 1: Executive Overview

The Working Holiday visa (subclass 417) is designed for young adults who want to combine an extended holiday in Australia with short-term work to fund their travels. This visa is part of Australia's Working Holiday Maker (WHM) program and offers pathways to second and third-year visas.

**Key Features:**
- **Duration:** 12 months initially
- **Age Limit:** 18-30 years (inclusive) - some countries 18-35
- **Work Rights:** Work for any employer up to 6 months each
- **Study Rights:** Up to 4 months study permitted
- **Cost:** AUD $635 (base application charge)
- **Processing:** Typically 10-35 days

**Second & Third Year Extensions:**
- Available after completing specified work in regional Australia
- 3 months specified work = Second year
- 6 months specified work = Third year

### General Eligibility Criteria

1. **Age Requirements**
   - Must be 18-30 years old at time of application (18-35 for some countries)
   - Must not have turned 31 (or 36) before application is decided

2. **No Dependent Children**
   - Must not have any dependent children accompanying you
   - Must not have dependent children at any time during stay

3. **Financial Requirements**
   - Sufficient funds to support yourself initially
   - Generally AUD $5,000 recommended
   - Plus funds for return/onward travel

4. **Health Requirements**
   - Meet health standards
   - May require health examination
   - Must not have tuberculosis

5. **Character Requirements**
   - No serious criminal history
   - May require police certificates
   - Must not have visa cancellations

6. **Previous WHM History**
   - Must not have previously entered Australia on a Working Holiday visa (417) unless applying for 2nd/3rd year
   - Must not have held a Work and Holiday (462) visa before

### Second Year Requirements

To qualify for a second Working Holiday visa:
- Hold or have held a first 417 visa
- Completed 3 months (88 days) of specified work in regional Australia
- Apply before first visa expires or within specified timeframe

### Third Year Requirements

To qualify for a third Working Holiday visa:
- Hold or have held a second 417 visa
- Completed 6 months of specified work in regional Australia on second visa

### Specified Work

Eligible specified work includes:
- Plant and animal cultivation
- Fishing and pearling
- Tree farming and felling
- Mining
- Construction

**Postcode Requirements:**
- Must be in designated regional areas
- Check eligible postcodes on DHA website
- Updated April 2025 with new disaster recovery areas

## Section 3: Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Check Eligibility
- Verify passport country eligibility
- Confirm age requirements
- Ensure no dependent children

### Step 2: Prepare Documents
- Valid passport
- Proof of funds
- Any required health/police checks

### Step 3: Create ImmiAccount
- Register at https://online.immi.gov.au
- Verify email address

### Step 4: Complete Application
- Select "New Application"
- Choose "Working Holiday Maker" → "Working Holiday (417)"
- Answer all questions accurately
- Declare health and character information

### Step 5: Pay Application Fee
- Base charge: AUD $635
- Additional charges for family members (if applicable)
- Payment via credit/debit card

### Step 6: Submit Application
- Upload all required documents
- Review application carefully
- Submit and note reference number

### Step 7: Biometrics (if required)
- Attend appointment if requested
- Book through VFS Global

### Step 8: Health Examination (if required)
- Complete through panel physician
- Results sent directly to DHA

### Step 9: Wait for Decision
- Monitor ImmiAccount
- Respond to any requests promptly

### Step 10: Receive Visa Grant
- Check visa conditions
- Note must-enter-by date
- Plan travel accordingly

## Section 8: Common Mistakes & Tips

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Incorrect Age Calculation**
   - Mistake: Applying after turning 31 (or 36)
   - Solution: Apply well before age limit

2. **Insufficient Specified Work Evidence**
   - Mistake: Poor record keeping for 2nd/3rd year
   - Solution: Keep detailed diary, pay slips, employer contacts

3. **Wrong Postcodes for Specified Work**
 - Mistake: Working in non-eligible areas
   - Solution: Check eligible postcodes before accepting work

4. **Dependent Children Oversight**
   - Mistake: Not declaring dependent children
   - Solution: Must have no dependents at any time during stay

5. **Insufficient Funds Evidence**
   - Mistake: No proof of financial capacity
   - Solution: Maintain bank statements showing $5,000+

### Expert Tips for Success

**Before Applying:**
- Check passport validity (6+ months)
- Research specified work areas if planning 2nd/3rd year
- Consider travel insurance

**During Your Stay:**
- Keep detailed records of all employment
- Don't exceed 6 months with one employer
- Track specified work days carefully
- Maintain health insurance

**For Second/Third Year:**
- Start specified work early
- Keep meticulous records
- Ensure work is in eligible postcodes
- Apply before first visa expires

### Understanding Visa Conditions

**Work Restrictions:**
- Maximum 6 months with any one employer
- No restrictions on type of work (except specified work for extensions)
- Can work for multiple employers

**Study Restrictions:**
- Maximum 4 months study
- Cannot be main purpose of visit

**Travel:**
- Multiple entries permitted
- Must enter Australia before "must not arrive after" date
